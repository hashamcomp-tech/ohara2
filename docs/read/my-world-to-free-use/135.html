“You will be working here from today. Your job is to tend to the mages in their training. What they require, you get it for them from the storage. The storage is over there.” A servant explained my duty to me as we went around a separate building that housed mage training rooms.

“When you go to the storage, you have to have a mage’s plaque with you, and you must make sure to inventory everything when you leave the storage. Thefts are not tolerated.” He warned me against having any funny ideas as he walked me through the storage where various herbs and cores were.

“What if I don’t take anything and something still goes missing?” I asked, just to be sure.

“Then you will all be punished.” The servant simply said before moving on.

“These are practice dummies. They regenerate when mages practice their skills on them. But they require a mana crystal to regenerate, so make sure to keep them fully stocked. That was the fate of a commoner after all.

“Also, this is for you.” He handed me a paper before he left, leaving me to do my tasks.

I unfolded the crude paper, which was made from some sort of special material, but not skin like commoners used, and read its contents.

“You are to come to the left wing of the castle at sundown every day.”

I had observed Zara’s writing enough by staying by her side for the last few weeks that I was well aware of her handwriting.

Barbaric times.

Twenty bucks, they were going to report this to the higher-ups.

Well, that was easier than I expected. Since things worked out so flawlessly, I didn’t remain on the topic anymore.
