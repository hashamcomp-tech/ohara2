The interface expanded in my vision, taking up more real estate than it usually did for standard notifications. Gold borders. Animated particles that looked like they’d been borrowed from a gacha game’s SSR pull animation. The System was putting on a show.

〘

Analysis: Subject Sloane Fitzgerald has achieved complete psychological and emotional investment in the Host. Her sense of self, happiness, and life trajectory are now fundamentally intertwined with Lukas Belmont. This bond transcends logic, circumstance, and external influence. The corruption is absolute.

My stomach did something complicated. Not quite nausea. Not quite triumph. Something in between that I didn’t have a word for.

Sloane loved me. She’d said it out loud. She’d meant it.

And the System had just reduced that love to a percentage and a completion notification. Quantified it. Filed it. Turned something that had felt raw and real in that room into data points and reward tiers.

〘

Reward Processing...

〙

The interface didn’t pause. Didn’t wait for me to catch up. It just kept going, line after line, like it was reading off a shopping receipt.

Three thousand points. Three Platinum pulls. A passive trait that sounded like it would do something permanent. Something called Bond Resonance that I didn’t have context for yet.

The System was paying out like I’d just hit max payout on a slot machine after feeding it coins for six straight hours. Which, from its perspective, I probably had. First heroine maxed out. Proof of concept established. The Scumbag’s Path officially validated by measurable, repeatable results.

I should have felt victorious. Should have felt like I’d won something.

Instead I felt like I needed to sit down and put my head between my knees before the hallway started spinning.

Host appears to be experiencing an emotional response to the reward distribution. This is noted but not relevant to the payout structure. Shall I proceed with the Gacha interface?

I read it through once. Then again, slower. Then a third time to make sure I wasn’t misunderstanding the language.

The System had just given me a permanent psychic link to Sloane. Not mind reading, nothing that invasive. Just awareness. The ability to sense her emotional state from anywhere in the world, and a flat stat boost when I was close to her.

〘

The Host seems troubled by this development. I would like to note that Devotion’s Echo is considered one of the most valuable passive traits in the Scumbag System’s reward architecture. Many Hosts would consider this payout exceptional.

〙

"Many Hosts aren’t dating the person the System just told them they’ve successfully corrupted."

〘

The terminology is clinical. The bond is genuine. These facts are not mutually exclusive.

〙

That was almost philosophical. For the System.

I leaned against the hallway wall, letting my head tip back against the plaster. Sloane’s door was maybe twenty feet behind me. I could still hear her moving around in her room, the soft shuffle and thump of her feet on the floor as she did whatever post-shower routine she’d established over the years. Getting dressed. Toweling off her hair. The ordinary sounds of someone who’d just had sex for the first time and was trying to process what that meant.

And now I could feel her too.

Not clearly. Not in words or images or anything that specific. Just warmth at the edge of my awareness, like standing near a fire I couldn’t see. A sense of contentment that didn’t belong to me. Satisfaction. Lingering pleasure. A thread of something softer underneath that might have been the love she’d just confessed out loud.

She was happy.

Because of me.

The System had quantified that happiness and packaged it into rewards, but the happiness itself wasn’t manufactured. Sloane Fitzgerald was genuinely content right now. She’d told me she loved me and meant it, and the aftermath of that confession was playing out in her as something I could now sense without her saying a word.

〘

The Host is engaging in emotional processing. This is acceptable. However, the Gacha interface is queued and I am required to inform you that Platinum-tier pulls have a time-sensitive bonus modifier that decreases by 0.1% per hour of delay. You currently have three queued.

〙

"You’re making that up."

〘

I am not making that up. The modifier is real. Whether you believe me is irrelevant to its function. You have three hours and forty-seven minutes before the first modifier reduction applies.

〙

I pushed off the wall. The warmth at the edge of my awareness shifted slightly, a flicker of something like curiosity. Sloane had noticed something. Maybe the sound of me moving in the hallway. Maybe just a vague awareness that I was still nearby.

The bond went both ways, apparently.

〘

It does not. The Heroine receives no direct awareness of the Host through Devotion’s Echo. What she is experiencing is standard emotional response to your proximity, unrelated to the passive trait.

"That’s almost reassuring."

Right. The Heroine Bond Resonance. Whatever that was separate from Devotion’s Echo.
