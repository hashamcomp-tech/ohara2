Four days. Lex meditated for four continuous days, doing his best to calm his mind. There were still a few issues here and there that required his attention, but since they were not emergencies, he ignored them.

Unfortunately, despite how critical the situation was, there was nothing more important than getting control over his own mental state. Lex could not even imagine the consequences of what would happen if he developed a Heart Demon while he was learning the Masterless technique.

The very fact that it took him four entire days to regain mental clarity and regain control over himself was a testament to just how incredibly stressed he really was. Maybe he had been dealing with his stress and dangerous situations for too long, and this was a sign for him to take things easy for a while.

Unfortunately, whether he took things easy or not was not in his control, at least for now.

Personally, Lex had another interpretation for why his mental state became so vulnerable. It was because he was playing too safe, taking too few risks, and taking up everything on his own shoulders. He needed to be much more serious about training his workers to be able to share his burden.

When he ended his meditation, Lex created another Innkeeper projection and looked at Z's progress. Stabilising space, without anchoring it to something, was not an easy endeavor. It was not connected to the realm boundary of the main Midnight realm, nor did it have the safety and stability of a treasure or piece of equipment.

It needed to exist on its own, be able to support the function of the laws that governed the land, resist instability, avoid collapse, and be able to merge seamlessly with other realms. That wasn't too much to ask, right?

Actually, it was still achievable, if barely, according to the original plan Lex had created for Z and the others, even if it took a long time. However, after receiving Taro's Journal, Lex had another idea. Instead of making this piece of space be able to fuse with any realm it was deployed to, he decided to have its stability target the Dream realm specifically.

For this reason, he had altered the plan given to Z, and had passed specific instructions to Charles as well. For whatever reason, Charles was very slow in producing usable viable information about the Dream realm. He had, however, been able to provide a decent amount of information about how to stabilise torn space and temporarily stitching it to a realm.

The purpose of the stitching in the information provided was to, eventually, perfectly fuse the torn space to a realm. Lex, however, did not need to do that.

"Are we ready to implement the next part of the torn space plan?" Lex asked Mary as he too observed the progress.

"Is a flame thrower sufficient to light a match? Of course everything will be ready if you throw so much money at it," she said. "I don't even know why you're worried about this at all."

Lex looked at her with a faint smile. She knew why he was so worried.

"This isn't the first time Midnight Inn workers have been trapped somewhere," said Mary, noting his silence. "Last time you went and saved everyone, and fell into a trap. Although everything worked out, it was still quite dangerous for you. That's why I'm pleased to see that you've learned from your previous experiences.

"This time everything will turn out well once again, and you don't need to do everything on your own either. Have a little faith in everyone else. The universe doesn't revolve around you, even if your system does."

"Look, I'm a little worried about a baby who got lost on her own. Sue me," said Lex as he tried to defend himself.

Mary's expression softened just a little.

"That baby has more protection wards on her than actual kings and rulers of massive empires. She'll be fine. The Dream realm may be dangerous, but it's not a death sentence. The chances of something going wrong are actually quite few. You'll see, everything will be fine when you guys finally get to her."

Lex nodded, and could only hope that Mary was right.

"Send the Valkyrie," he said, letting Mary handle the task.

Whenever they had work, they would go and do it, but they spent every free moment that they had in this chamber, recovering. After all, other than Shireen, the others had even lost their sense of identities. They wanted to recover that as soon as possible.
