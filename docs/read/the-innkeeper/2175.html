Lex took a deep breath, and then sighed. Of course the quest was not simple, or easy. Unfortunately, Lex was a chronic overachiever, so hearing about the new parameters by which the quest would be judged, he already knew where he was going to establish the resort for maximum benefit.

Yet actually deploying it might have to wait until Lex was free from learning the Masterless Technique. He couldn't be sure because if he took too long to learn the technique, he might be forced to use alternative means.

At least he could begin making preparations for the resort beforehand. Speaking of which, he would need to strengthen the space of the resort if he was going to carry out his plans. For that, he would need to get his hands on those Star Dust Dandelions, which could help him create a strong, independent space. The normal timeline for that was a thousand years, if he recalled correctly, and that certainly wouldn't work for him.

After thinking about it for a moment, he decided he could give the dandelions ten years to mature. Now he just needed to figure out how to do such a thing. As it happened, there was a certain turtle who had been clamoring to meet the Innkeeper anyway.

"Mary, send in the turtle," said Lex as he took his seat and prepared to argue against the Sovereign. Since he was aware of what was happening inside the Inn, he could more or less guess what issue the turtle had encountered. It was truly a case of suffering from success.

It did not take long before the turtle arrived inside the office, glancing around for a moment to evaluate it.

Both of these plants were original creations of the turtle, made upon the request of certain guests. Who knew they'd be so popular once they were made, not to mention valuable?
