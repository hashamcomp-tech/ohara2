The upgrade in Midnight Inn level was quite straightforward, even if he hadn't seen an upgrade in a while. It basically increased his authority to do things within his system which was nice. The higher the authority, the fewer the restrictions.

The other things, though, were quite interesting, and required him to check them out.

Life level Opportunity token

An upgraded version of the Unique opportunity token - it allows the Host to attain an opportunity graded at Life level. Only one such token exists in the universe, so use appropriately. Warning: Some Life level opportunities may attract unwanted attention, so choose cautiously.

Additional Note: Dao materials are ranked as Destiny/Fate -> Divine/Oblivion >> Death/Spirit -> Life/Chaos.

Lex was surprised to see the additional note, but quickly understood that he was given the knowledge because of his increased authority. Also, this was the

first time he heard that Dao materials were ranked.

That would explain why everyone thought his payment to attend the tea party was so cheap since he didn't specify the rank of the material required. He found the ranking to be odd though.

From what he could tell, there were four levels of ranking, but there could be two names per rank. So Destiny and Fate could refer to the same rank of Dao material, which was odd. However, what was clear was that the Divine and Oblivion were above Destiny and Fate, and the same was true for each subsequent rank.

Perhaps there were certain nuances within the materials which would allow them to align better with one specific title over the other - he wasn't quite sure. However, this did bring some clarity to the quest ranks that he used to get.

He tried to figure out what the pattern was between the ranks, but wasn't able to discover a pattern that fit all ranks. For example, Life and Death were clearly related, but they were at two different ranks. However, Fate and Destiny were on the same rank. Based on this pattern, Lex expected to see Order since there was also Chaos, but in its place there was Oblivion. Divine and Spirit also seemed to lack their opposite, and he couldn't figure out why Spirit was above Divine even though Divine energy was one of the most potent types of energy in existence.

There was a lot to think about regarding these ranks, but that would have to wait as Lex was too interested in the rest of the rewards.

Innkeeper's Scent Diffuser

The Innkeeper's scent diffuser is the perfect tool to create a warm and welcoming environment for guests visiting his office. The diffuser works based on the level of Dao material entered inside it, creating unique and enchanting scents based on each material.

As a side effect of creating the perfect ambience, the scent from the diffuser also has a chance of causing a Dao related enlightenment among peak Celestial and Demi-Dao Lords, increasing the chances of them forming a Dao Heart. It can also promote comprehension of their own Dao among newborn Dao Lords. Oh, right. It was a tool for creating ambience. Got it. As a side note, it could also increase chances of forming a Dao heart and promote Dao comprehension and stuff, but that wasn't anything important. Got it, got it.

Lex immediately summoned the Innkeeper's Scent Diffuser and placed it on his table, taking a good look at it. It looked like a tree spiraling out of a point with the very peak of the tree right above the centre of the pond.

What was interesting about the diffuser was that it could work even with ingredients below Dao rank, though the effects would naturally be lacking. Even so, Lex took one of the peak Celestial level materials he conned from Wu Kong and put it inside the pond of the diffuser.

Immediately the material dissolved, and a thick white smoke covered the surface of the pond.

Slowly, the smoke started to travel up the tree, spreading out across the room as it did. Lex sniffed it, and noticed that it really did smell nice.

In fact, the smell reminded him of the feeling of being in good health - of being able to run, to walk, to...

Without realizing it, Lex fell into an enlightenment state relating to health and recovery. Many of the laws he had recently learned about from Regix started to make sense to him, and their nuances began to make sense to him.
