Chapter 1730: Bonus Active

All of the summons were dismissed, and the beasts returned to their spaces.

Reluctantly on the part of Cara and Remi, who both wanted to explore this place a bit more, even though they’d already been checking the area for anything worth taking and found nothing.

However, Remi had bamboo to plant in the swamp of her space. That was the priority, as it was a high-grade resource, and a new plant that she didn’t have. She had other types of bamboo, but this one was special, and was considered a rare grade resource by the system.

The team stepped back out of the portal, where a few groups were still staging, waiting for their time to enter.

There was only a one-minute limit between groups, so mostly it was a matter of planning and making sure everyone had what they needed.

The final boss was a nightmare for most groups, despite being the easiest of the three for Karl’s group.

When the last of them stepped outside, a System notice appeared in the air over the portal.

{New Boss Kill record time set by the New Home Merchant Clan}

{Ironman Bonus activated} 60 minutes of bonus rewards from repeated clearances.

Everyone stared up at the message, then at the group who had just exited.

"Well, it looks like we’re turning around and going back inside," Dana noted in a dry tone.

Elder Socrate sighed. "I think that you’re right. We will have to discuss the payment for the extra runs afterward, since we’re on a timer. The Telheusto Elders will surely understand that it’s a system bonus."

Karl nodded, and immediately turned to enter the portal again.

The visitors to the portal began to cheer as they realized what had happened. The boss clear records rarely changed, so most forgot that it could happen without sending a group of Clan Leaders to deliberately alter it to see if it gave rewards.

Once they were back inside, Karl saw that the dungeon was fully reset, but the lighting had changed, and there was a large timer over the top of the centre of the instance.

"Alright, this is going to get messy. We will rush straight for the bosses, while the Golems and summons clear the rest of the dungeon. Not a full clear, straight through. Even if we miss a few, it shouldn’t hurt the loot too much.

Don’t spend the time to randomize, just grab and go, the loot should be good enough with the bonus."

Elder Alix smiled as Karl began issuing orders.

The Pet Sitter was a natural-born leader, but some of that was likely from growing up with a team of beasts that needed directions to prevent them going off and doing their own thing.

Especially the badger.

The new bears appeared, every bit as intimidating as summons as they had been as the boss. They were even the same size, not a degraded version. That was going to be a nasty shock for anything that they came across.

"All the boss monsters let loose on the dungeon. First The Karl, then Oathbreaker, Thor, and now the Tyrants." Cara cheered.

Karl laughed as he made a shooing motion for the badger to join the teams clearing the mobs ahead of them. They needed a clear path to the boss if they weren’t going to be delayed too much.

That didn’t seem likely.

There were too many groups along the main route to the boss, and the woods were the same. If you entered, you pulled more monsters. So, they were going to have to do some fighting.

However, the summoned creatures had split up the same as they did for the bear section.

Split into four groups, they could fight their way forward smoothly, and leapfrog each other to keep the clear going.

"Change of plans, finish the clear as we go. Soon, things will only need a few hits to finish them when we arrive, and I’ve got Mana Pylons, so there is no need to hold back on the heavy damage skills."

The Spirit Bears roared happily as they reached combat, and Dana shook her head in dismay.

"This isn’t even going to be a fight. The majority of the dungeon is completely incapable of damaging them. Even without Limited Invulnerability, they are immune to physical damage."

Elder Alix sighed. "I wish I could learn that skill. That is absolutely broken. Now, don’t get me wrong, this new amulet is spectacular. But having that bear to take the damage for me would be so much better."

"Well, you know what Elder to take with you now. And soon, more of the druid and other compatible classes will have it," Dana joked.

She wasn’t actually certain how many classes could even use the summoning skill for the Spirit Bear Tyrant. But some of the Druid Related ones certainly should be able to. It was the sort of skill that their class gave them at lower power levels.

The bears also did an impressive amount of damage. Not as much as Oathbreaker, especially when they had multiple groups engaged, and his damage spreading skill would hit everything.

But more than any of the other Golems, despite their defensive focus.

With the damage focused, and everyone burning through mana to clear faster, they nearly marched through the first group. It was under ten seconds before they were finished, and the group was moving forward again, jogging to the second pack.

[Indomitable], Leo’s skill to prevent exhaustion, was going to be invaluable for this run, as everyone was going to be moving fast.

If Karl’s mental calculation was right, they should be able to get two full runs in under an hour, and might even get started on a third. That could add up to a lot of bonus loot.

The Clan only expected them to do one today, so three was already good. Three with better than average loot would make everyone happy.

Everyone but Cara, who wanted to roll for better loot at every box. Even if it reduced the quantity.
