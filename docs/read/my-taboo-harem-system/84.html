"Sophia! Sophia!"

She kept knocking, calling her name over and over as though that would somehow make the door open.

Besides, going to work wasn’t entirely pointless.

Not only was he making money, but he was also trying to get his abilities under control, using Antonia as a sort of training guide.

And so, when Denzel arrived at work, he stepped into the massage parlor and shut the door behind him.

As he turned around, he spotted Antonia standing behind the counter, casually reading through a stack of papers.

Her eyes briefly flickered toward him before returning to the document in her hands.

Then she froze.

A second later, she slowly looked up again.

Her gaze immediately landed on the bandages wrapped around his arm.

"What happened to you?"

Denzel paused and let out a small sigh. Before he could even answer, Antonia’s eyes widened.

"You got into a fight?"

Her expression twisted with disbelief.

"What the hell is wrong with you?"

Without waiting for an explanation, she marched around the counter, grabbed Denzel by his left arm, and dragged him deeper into the building.

The door clicked shut behind them.

A few moments later, they found themselves inside one of the private massage rooms, completely alone.

Denzel rolled his eyes, annoyed by her escalation.

"Don’t judge me. I just came here to do my job."

"Your job?"

Antonia shot him an incredulous look.

"Well, I can’t exactly let you get on with your job when your hands are injured."

She folded her arms beneath her chest and stared directly at him.

"Do you even know if you’re capable of performing any real massage techniques in that condition?"

Denzel opened his mouth to respond as he flashed his all so confident grin at her.

"Do you want to try and see?"

Antonia frowned.

"I’m being serious."

She folded her arms.

"You’re my employee. I can’t just disregard something like this."

"Don’t worry," Denzel replied with a dismissive wave.

"I’ll be fine."

"Yeah?"

Antonia scoffed before extending a hand toward him.

"Give me your hands."

Denzel blinked, unsure of why she’d requested that.

"Why?"

"Let’s not get ahead of ourselves. We still have training to do."

Antonia rolled her eyes.

"You’re a newbie. You need constant energy control just to manipulate mana properly."

She pointed at the bandages wrapped around his palms.

"You could barely control anything when you were perfectly healthy, and now you think you’re going to do it with damaged hands?"

Denzel clicked his tongue.

’Tsk. That means another drawback, as expected.’

He had honestly hoped his injuries wouldn’t affect his training.

"What good is showing you my hands going to do?" he asked.

"I’ll heal them."

Denzel froze.

"Heal me?"

His eyebrows shot upward.

"You can do that?"

Antonia nodded.

"It’s part of my specialty."

"Specialty?"

Denzel tilted his head.

"Well well, that doesn’t really answer anything. How exactly do you do it?"

For the first time since he’d arrived, Antonia hesitated.

A faint blush crept onto her cheeks.

At the same time, a trace of annoyance flickered through her eyes.

She remained silent momentarily as Denzel stared at her.

"What’s with the sudden silence?"

"Ah..."

Antonia looked away.

"It’s complicated."

"Complicated? Enlighten me..."

Denzel narrowed his eyes suspiciously.

"My specialty follows different principles," she muttered.

Denzel continued staring at her.

The longer he stared, the more uncomfortable she became.

Eventually, Antonia let out a long sigh and rolled her eyes.

"Fine."

She rubbed her forehead.

"For me to heal someone, I have to fulfill a certain criteria first."

Denzel raised an eyebrow.

"And that is?"

Antonia’s blush deepened.

For a moment, she looked as though she regretted bringing the subject up at all.

Then, after another reluctant sigh, she finally answered.

"I need to kiss the person first."
