I whistled. Expensive as hell. But with Limit Breaker, I could train my stats naturally—it would just take time. Maybe spending points on direct stat upgrades wasn’t the smartest move.

"What about upgrading my stolen abilities?"

That seemed more reasonable. For 1,500 points, I could upgrade all three of my stolen abilities from Copper to Bronze. Or I could take one to Silver. But which one would give me the most bang for my buck?

Wave Motion was my only offensive ability. It let me shoot energy blasts and boost my mobility, but it drained stamina like crazy at Copper rank.

Treasure Sense was utility—finding valuable items up to five meters away. Useful but not game-changing yet.

And now I had Aurora’s Sensory Hijack. The ability to manipulate someone’s five senses through touch. At Copper rank, it was limited to one sense at a time and required constant concentration, but even that was powerful. With a touch, I could make someone taste something that wasn’t there, hear phantom sounds, or feel sensations I wasn’t actually creating.

The strategic applications were fucking endless. Combat. Infiltration. Seduction. Interrogation.

Well, that was a no-brainer. With two-person capacity, I could bring both Belle and Naomi into the Sanctum together. That opened up... possibilities.

Another easy decision. Doubling my storage capacity would let me stockpile more essence, giving me reserves for emergencies or Overclock fuel.

That was tempting. Two extra minutes in a fight could mean the difference between life and death.

I stretched, feeling the delicious ache in my muscles. Aurora had ridden me hard, leaving scratches on my back and a satisfied warmth in my chest. What a fucking day. Seven days ago I was fat, pathetic, and days from death. Now I had a squad, multiple abilities, and three gorgeous women letting me drink milk from their tits.

Life was weird.

The System had given me infinite potential through Limit Breaker. I could surpass any genetic ceiling, become faster, stronger, more capable than any normal hunter—but it would take months or years of consistent training. Time I didn’t have if I wanted to beat Blair Davenport.

So what should I prioritize? Surviving long-term meant upgrading my abilities to be more effective at extraction and combat. But winning against Blair meant getting stronger physically, fast.

I cracked my knuckles, making my decision.
