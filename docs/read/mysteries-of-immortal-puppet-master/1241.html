Chapter 1241: Chapter 480: Heart Fire Essence

The location of the Heart Fire Essence contest was set in a valley at the bottom of a cliff.

As dusk settled, the mountains reflected a deep, golden glow.

Seventy-two glazed lamps cast golden reflections on the cold pool, while ghost orchids wept dewdrops.

Li Guanyu, dressed in a blue robe, leaned against a pine tree branch, lightly tapping a bronze bell with a jade ruler hidden in his sleeve.

The bell’s toll commanded the scene. Li Guanyu spoke, "You have passed the first three spirit paper trials and should know the method of making spirit paper lies in rectifying one’s heart and understanding the Heavenly Dao. However, the Heart Fire Essence to be refined today requires incorporating paradoxical materials into the traditional alchemy."

"These materials devour the heart, akin to wielding a double-edged sword. Should the Divine Sense waver, causing monstrosities to appear in the flesh, one must learn from the ancients who had the wisdom to sever a limb..."

Li Guanyu unusually spoke in a stern tone, forewarning everyone of specific areas that required special attention.

Unlike the previous spirit paper, the Heart Fire Essence employs paradoxical materials. The more these materials are used, the greater the negative impact they have on the cultivator’s body and mind.

Li Guanyu continued explaining the method of producing the Heart Fire Essence: "Heart Fire Essence is the essence spirit of Li Palace, the heterogeneous nature of Bing and Ding. Its form is formless, its nature is ever-changing, a dust in the void created by the battle of Yin Yang..."

All the cultivators perked their ears, focused, and listened carefully.

Gu Qing stood with lowered eyes, listening to the refining method of Heart Fire Essence, comparing it to what he had learned, checking for any discrepancies, while also using his Divine Sense to observe the various materials before him.

The materials were divided into three categories, clearly distinguished.

The first category was Confucian Cultivation materials.

There was a Heart Inquiry Lotus with seven petals. This spiritual material was born in Wenqu Pond Water, and after being bathed in the recitations of scholars for three hundred years, it blossomed with seven petals. The petals showed a jade-like quality, revealing the micro-carvings of Confucian classics in daylight and echoing the recitations of sages at night under the moon dew.

There was a handful of Bright Text Grass. No more than three inches tall, it had nine leaves growing in a circle. The leaf veins showed a silver thread chessboard grid, within each grid, text could be written, making it ideal for spirit paper treasure.

There was also a bottle of Wenxin Tears. This was no ordinary water dew, but rather the essence tears produced from the depths of literary emotion in the Literary Heart of a Confucian Cultivator. It resembled amber, containing a rainbow glow, and when shaken, the virtual shadows of the classics like "Poetry" and "Book" swirled within.

The second category was Monster Path materials.

Several Chaotic Ink Stones, as big as sparrow eggs. They were soft like molasses during Yin Hour, harder than Mystic Iron at Noon, seeping foul ink at You Hour, suddenly sprouting bone spikes at Zi Hour.

Delusional Spider Silk, too fine to be seen, only visible under the dilation of a cultivator’s pupils during Zi Hour, drifting like a wandering soul. Upon contact, it was icy cold, but after three breaths, it caused a burning pain.

There was also Inverted Cinnabar, colored like solidified blood scabs. To ordinary sight, it looked regular, but reflected in water, its true form appeared as a writhing creature, its mirror image dispersing rainbow mist.

The last category was regular auxiliary materials.

A bowl of Rootless Water. The bowl was made of pure silver, with the Twenty-Eight Constellations Diagram engraved inside. The water rhythmically revealed Thunder Patterns, capable of being stored for three years without spoilage.

Jade Marrow Powder. It revealed gold powder-like star points when exposed to light, and when scattered in the wind, it transformed into a phoenix shape, soaring upwards.

Five-Colored Spirit Soil, roughly proportioned according to the River Map numbers of east three, south four, west six, north one, and center nine, useful for harmonizing Yin Yang and the Five Elements.

As the pine branch trembled slightly, Li Guanyu lightly tapped the jade ruler in his hand, causing the seventy-two glazed lamps to suddenly flare three times brighter, "Let’s begin."

Upon hearing this, Gu Qing immediately took action.

Confucian Skill—Observing Things and Gaining Knowledge!

In an instant, his eyes shone brightly, Literary Energy gushing forth, billowing his robe.

In his Upper Dantian Divine Sea, his Literary Palace stood tall, and the virtual image of the Heart Inquiry Lotus rapidly emerged, becoming actualized at a visible speed.

Soon after, the nine-leaf chessboard grid of the Mingxin Grass unfolded layer by layer, and the virtual image of the Wenxin Tears was analyzed, revealing a seven-colored rainbow light.

Gu Qing was a true Confucian Cultivator, a genius famed across the nation. These Confucian orthodox materials appeared to him like a meticulous white sketch with its skeletal structure removed, where every stroke of the brush was clear and discernible.

He understood them completely.

As for the regular auxiliary materials, he also quickly analyzed them thoroughly, much like a skilled butcher dissecting an ox: "The Thunder Pattern in the Rootless Water is intact, the gold powder in the Jade Marrow Powder is excellent, and the Five-Colored Soil aligns with the River Map numbers—all of these are good."

However, when his Divine Sense touched the Monster Path materials in an attempt to analyze them.

In Gu Qing’s Literary Palace, the speed of the material virtual image’s condensation sharply dropped.

"Crack." A beam within the Literary Palace suddenly twisted into a spiral.

First was the Chaotic Ink Stone.

Its virtual image, once formed, exuded a cloyingly sweet murderous intent like molasses, then suddenly shifted to a hardened frenzy akin to Mystic Iron.

Next was the Delusional Spider Silk.

Countless thoughts rose and fell, emerging in Gu Qing’s Upper Dantian, then wantonly washing over it, shaking his lofty Literary Palace until it was teetering.

Lastly, the Inverted Cinnabar.

Gu Qing saw overlapping phantom illusions, his five senses bewildered and unordered, though he stood on stone ground, it felt as if he had fallen into an abyss, his entire body shivered with an extreme chill.

Gu Qing lowered his head, stifling a groan, with trails of blood at the corners of his eyes.

These virtual images of Monster Path materials had become nearly substantial, yet they still fell short of the actual objects.

Gu Qing fully understood: Any time Monster Path treasures were involved, this was the way of things. Unless one specially cultivated in the Monster Path, no one from any other school could completely analyze Monster Path materials.

Gu Qing began processing the materials.

This time, he was more prepared as three Magic Artifacts flew out from his sleeve.

The Green Jade Grinder floated above the Heart Inquiry Lotus, and as its wheel turned, the lotus petals peeled off by themselves, transforming the jade quality into a milky fluid.

The Silver Thread Screen enveloped the Bright Text Grass, which trembled within the screen, turning into silver powder.

Gu Qing chanted a spell, and in response, the Wenxin Tears shattered upon hearing it, rainbow rays streaming like rivulets into the slurry of the first two materials, immediately producing a melodious reading sound.
