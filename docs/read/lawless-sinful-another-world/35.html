After returning to the inn once, I picked up the borrowed clothes and visited the Adventurers' Guild for the first time in about a week.

Unlike that time, no one was staring at me. Lisa wasn’t next to me either. And the clothes I was wearing were completely different from back then.

“Long time no see, Takuma-san.”

But for someone like Lorraine who works at the reception desk, the number of adventurers who come in each day surely exceeds double digits. And when thinking on a weekly basis, the number of people registering as adventurers like I did is probably not small either.

To me, Lorraine might be a special person, but from her point of view, I’m just one ordinary visitor among many to the guild.

That’s why I was deeply moved that she called my name first.

It was essentially like an item box— a super-dimensional pocket that could hold anything up to a certain capacity.

I didn’t buy this storage bag. I received it from Bottas, who had been using it before.

When I received the money the other day, I didn’t have anything to carry it in, so Bottas thoughtfully gave it to me.

On top of that, I hadn’t visited the guild once in the past week. Meaning I hadn’t done even a single copper coin’s worth of work as an adventurer, yet I had changed this much. It was understandable that someone like Lorraine would reach that conclusion.

Even in my original world, if someone who had barely shown up to their part-time job for a month suddenly appeared wearing expensive stuff, people would ask where the money came from.

“Alright. I’ll tell you what I was doing this past week.”

With a slight intention of bragging about my exploits, I told Lorraine about my achievements over the past week.
