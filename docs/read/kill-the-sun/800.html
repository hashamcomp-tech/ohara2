Chapter 800

Nick finished the last years of his time off working with the Technician.

While he wasn't very interested in how the Technician's machines worked, he needed to act like he was doing the Envious Researcher's job.

After the last few years of his free time had passed, Nick retreated to an isolated location.

"Oh, Sun," Nick said, activating the recording function of the Sun. "This is Julian Winter, the replacement for the Envious Researcher."

"I have joined the Technician in his research for several years, and I am here to report on humanity's progress."

"Humanity has created a nuclear fusion reactor that creates a small star inside of it. The reactor is stable without any issues. During the creation of the star, the Technician managed to harvest a new kind of energy. The energy is toxic to humans and very unstable."

"Since it is made by a small star, I assume this is the Star Energy you were talking about. As part of my duties, I directed the research into the direction you wanted."

"I believe that an abundance of Star Energy would make humanity progress much faster in developing new technologies. The faster humanity progresses, the worse it is for them since it will destroy the balance between technology and personal power."

"I have redirected the focus of the research from producing more Star Energy to making use of Star Energy. I argued that the most powerful Specters have already dealt with Star Energy in the past, which means they can counter it."

"Humanity is currently researching ways to transform Star Energy into small weapons that can be used by Extractors. Swords, rifles, spears, and so on."

"Humanity needs more personal power, and upgraded equipment will help in this regard."

"When humanity manages to create powerful personal weapons with Star Energy, I will redirect the research towards biological enhancement."

"You have said that humanity's talent in altering biology is not as good as its talent to turn cold steel into powerful weapons. I will redirect humanity towards creating an alternative of Zephyx Synchronizers."

"Star Energy can be used as a source of power, and having a source of power that inherently counters Specters will help during battles."

"However, humans are designed to work with Zephyx, not Star Energy. Attuning all of a human's body so that it works with Star Energy instead of Zephyx will take a considerable amount of time."

"I'll have to see where I should go after that, and I might need your guidance when it is time, but that will still be centuries from now."

"That is my report. If you wish for me to alter humanity's development further, please instruct me."

Nick took a deep breath and stopped talking.

His ability had not deactivated during his entire speech, which told him that nobody was looking at him.

This confirmed once again that the Sun was not sentient.

"I'm ready to get back to work," Nick said.

The Left Arm nodded. "You know what you have to do."

"Contribute," Nick said.

'I'm not sure if I should deal with Sloth,' Nick thought as he walked towards the exit of Aegis' headquarters. 'Sloth has essentially conquered the entire Grand Continent, but its impact is limited due to its modus operandi.'

Nick stopped walking.

"Champion, I have a question," he asked out loud in a random corridor.

Nick had never contacted the Champion like this, but due to his new job, he believed that he had the right.

"Ask," the Champion's voice spoke in Nick's mind.

"Are the Trio of Destruction on the same level?" he asked.

"No," the Champion answered. "War is the strongest. It is most likely a Late Adversary. Famine is a Mid Adversary, but it might advance to the level of Late Adversary soon. Pestilence is the weakest. It became an Early Adversary a couple of centuries ago."

Nick nodded. "I will work towards strengthening Pestilence. Since there is only a limited amount of resources, strengthening Pestilence means weakening the growth of the other two."

"I'm going to deal with Gluttony," Nick explained.

"You have proven effective and efficient," the Champion answered. "I will leave it up to you."

"Thank you, Champion," Nick said before walking out of Aegis' headquarters.
