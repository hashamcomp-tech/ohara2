Chapter 767

A red-headed human Specter was watching a city from far away.

It looked like it was planning to make an attack.

Based on its power, it was a Late Demon, which meant its power was nothing to sneer at.

If it actually decided to attack the city, it had a chance to succeed.

Yet, after looking at the city for a couple of hours, it just turned around and left.

It was too risky.

The Specter shot through the wasteland for a couple of hours before it stopped in front of another city.

There, it waited for another couple of hours before leaving.

Also too dangerous.

It kept going from city to city for almost an entire day, moving towards the west the entire time.

After looking at nearly twenty cities over the span of several days, it suddenly stopped in the middle of the wasteland.

The next moment, it turned into a human who activated his Barrier.

The human released a sigh of relief.

'I'm finally outside its perception range,' Nick thought.

Yes, the Specter that turned into a human was Nick.

What had been Nick's plan during these couple of days?

Not dying!

That was his plan!

Shortly before Nick had reached the next city on his rampage of dealing with Specters, his ability deactivated, and a terrifying sense locked onto him.

Luckily, Nick had been prepared for such an eventuality, which was why he always moved from city to city while being disguised.

'To think that my actions have such a huge impact. I thought that there would be a chance, but I wouldn't have thought that it would actually happen,' Nick thought in relief as he further traveled to the west, disguised as a human this time.

So, who had been looking at Nick?

It wasn't a Demon.

It wasn't a Fallen.

It wasn't War's new servant.

No, it had been something much more terrifying.

Since Nick could sense where the thing was that was looking at him, he also knew what it was based on its location.

The thing that was looking at him came from the east coast of the Great Triangle, very close to Aegis' headquarters.

It was not the Champion of Light.

There was something else there that was just as powerful as the Champion of Light.

'My actions even attracted Death,' Nick thought.

Yes, Death had moved.

It moved from deep within the ocean to its coast.

That expanded the range of its senses a lot.

'I can't think of another reason why Death would move closer to the coast. Something like this probably hasn't happened before.'

It was important to remember that Death had the power to kill any Extractor who moved through the east of the Great Triangle.

It was also important to remember that it wasn't the Champion of Light keeping Death locked down but the other way around.

Death was why the Champion of Light couldn't leave.

As long as it wasn't seen, Death could move around in the vicinity of Aegis' headquarters.

Aegis' headquarters just needed to remain within striking distance.
