Chapter 784

After handing the book to Nick, the Sun vanished again.

For the first time in his life, Nick had come into contact with the actual Sun, or more precisely, with some kind of alien using it.

It was clear that the Sun wasn't sentient. There was no doubt about it.

It was almost omnipresent, and it could look at anyone at any given time.

The fact that it didn't look at Nick until he came out of the Containment Unit was proof enough that it wasn't sentient.

Additionally, it had only started paying attention to them almost half an hour after they killed the Envious Researcher.

A sentient, omnipresent being would have just turned its head to look.

'I expected this would happen at some point,' Nick thought. 'I needed to become an agent of the Sun at some point. Specters are creations of the Sun, and there are bound to be restrictions on them.'

'Without its blessing, I wouldn't be able to advance at some point.'

'I was planning on becoming an agent at some point, but it makes no difference if it is now or later.'

'I have one more mask, but nothing really changed.'

'In fact, only positive things came of this.'

"We are working for the same being now," the Champion commented. "However, nothing much has changed. You are still part of Aegis."

Nick nodded. "The Sun is humanity's ally, and Aegis represents humanity. Between the two, there is not much difference in whom I work for. It all comes down to the same thing, humanity's rise."

The Champion nodded. "As I have stated in the past, the Sun is not humanity's enemy, but its ally. Our duty to the Sun is even more important than our duty to Aegis. Aegis represents only one era, while the Sun represents all of humanity's history."

"Agreed," Nick said. "As instructed, I will not share any of the information I have heard today. I will immediately get onto my new job. The Envious Researcher just died, and the Technician is probably preparing to research all kinds of things."

The Champion looked at Nick. "A word of caution. The Specters are still our enemies despite being the creations of the Sun."

"I understand," Nick answered. "They are a trial for humanity. The trial would be worthless if they didn't give their best to stop humanity."

"As long as you understand," the Champion said.

Nick performed a quick and polite bow. "I will return to my duties, Champion."

The Champion just nodded, and the isolation function of the office was deactivated.

Nick stepped out of the Champion's office and entered the Left Arm's office.

Naturally, the Left Arm had returned by now.

"Anything I need to know?" she asked.

"Nothing has changed," Nick said. "I just received a commendation and another couple of years of leave from my duties."

The Left Arm nodded. "Tell me when you are ready to return to work."

Nick just nodded before leaving.

The Left Arm was beyond intelligent, and she could hear Nick's secret message.

Nothing had changed.

When talking in secret, no unnecessary words were said.

Unnecessary words clouded the message.

The fact that Nick opened with that sentence meant only one thing.

His loyalties still lay with the Technician and the Left Arm.

Of course, it could also be that Nick's loyalties had shifted and that he knew what to say to placate the Left Arm's suspicions.

Both were possible.

But the Left Arm trusted Nick.

She knew where Nick came from.

She knew his origin and how much he desired to help humanity.

This was not just a random Specter.

This was Nick.

Additionally, the fact that Nick said that he received a commendation and a couple of years of free time was also an obvious lie.

The Champion never gave Nick any free time.

All of it was approved by the Left Arm.

And a commendation?

From the Champion?

He really didn't seem like a person who would give praise to a Specter.

Naturally, the Left Arm was right with her suspicion.

The Champion didn't give him any free time.

Nick just lied and said that he got a couple of years so that he could benefit more.

Obviously, Nick was supposed to come up with a cover story as to why he talked to the Champion in private, and he used this lie as his cover.

What was the Champion going to do? Tell the Left Arm that he didn't grant Nick any free time?

That would make things even more suspicious.

Nick rarely told the Left Arm what he was doing, and the fact that he did was a big clue.

Trust.
