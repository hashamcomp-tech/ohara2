Damon stood at the entrance of Selene’s mansion. He looked down the misty path that led into the core disciple court. The air felt cool against his skin.

His golden eyes scanned the surroundings for any sign of movement.

A young outer disciple walked by carrying a stack of scrolls. Damon stepped forward and stopped the boy with a raised hand. The disciple froze and bowed low.

"Where is the library," Damon asked. His voice stayed calm and direct.

The boy pointed toward a stone bridge on the left. He explained the path wound past three pavilions and ended at a tall building with blue tiles.

Damon nodded once and turned away. The disciple hurried off without another word.

Damon followed the directions. His black robes brushed against his legs as he walked. He passed flowering trees that glowed faintly under the morning light.

This place holds answers the system never gave me. I need to know how the current realms work and what the different the memories I got.

He crossed the stone bridge. Water flowed beneath it with a steady sound. Damon kept his pace even. His mind stayed focused on the task ahead.

Two disciples stood near the next pavilion. They noticed his robe and stepped aside. Damon walked past them without slowing.

Their whispers followed him but he paid no attention.

The path opened into a wide courtyard. A tall building rose at the far end. Blue tiles covered the roof and silver runes marked the doors.

Damon stopped for a moment at the entrance and get inside immediately.

An elder sat behind a desk inside the first hall. Damon approached and showed his inner disciple token. The elder glanced at it and gestured toward the rows of shelves.

"Inner disciples may access the upper floors," the elder said.

Damon gave a short nod. He moved deeper into the library. Shelves stretched high above him filled with thick tomes.

The air smelled of old paper and spiritual ink. He started to explore the library.

Then he pulled down a a couple books that have what he need then he found a chair and table to sit an read. Damon began flipped through the pages.

As he delved into the dusty volumes, time slipped away, unnoticed by Damon. The heavy, lingering silence of the library wrapped around him, yet he remained entirely captivated by the new information.
