Chapter 290

On the other side, surrounded by little fairies, Leon gradually noticed that the colors before his eyes were condensing, transforming into one dreamlike giant tree after another, each bearing all kinds of strange and wonderful fruits.

Beneath these great fruit trees stood plump, oversized mushrooms. Their stems had small doors carved into them, and their caps were dotted with palm-sized holes that looked like windows.

As his vision became clearer, Leon also saw countless crystal-clear vines, intertwined like glass, forming statues of various animals. There were small arched stone houses built on the short grass, and besides the fairies circling around him, he also spotted another familiar creature.

The Junimos—round and cute, like tiny apples!

“You’ve come.”

While Leon was still looking around, a kindly old man’s voice suddenly sounded by his ear.

“I’ve come.”

Leon answered instinctively. Then, following the voice, he looked around, but saw no one.

“You shouldn’t have come.”

“But I still came.”

The old man’s voice sounded again, and Leon responded without thinking. He couldn’t help it—the exchange was all too familiar, like a script they had rehearsed in advance, each line falling into place naturally.

“What did you come here for?”

Just as Leon expected, the next line came right on cue. He continued the “script.”

“I came to kill.”

At this point, Leon was certain he had fallen into a deep hallucination. Otherwise, how could someone in this world be quoting lines from an ancient martial arts novel? It had to be his subconscious at work.

But then, unexpectedly, the old man didn’t follow the script. Instead, he replied in a surprised tone:

“You still dare to kill?”

“Huh?”

Leon was stunned. The conversation in his hallucination had actually changed. Now he didn’t know if he should stick to the lines.

Before he could figure out a response, he felt a sudden thump on the back of his head—a flick to the skull. Whoever did it had controlled their strength, but it still hurt.

“Why did you hit me?”

Leon rubbed the sore spot, annoyed. This didn’t make sense—hallucinations weren’t supposed to come with pain, like in dreams. So why did that hurt?

“What, should I have given you a hug instead? Did you hear yourself just now?” The old man’s voice grew irritable, clearly displeased with Leon’s answers.

“Aren’t we just going through the lines? By the way, where is this exactly? Is it my hallucination, or somewhere else entirely?”

Leon was beginning to suspect that this wasn’t an illusion after all, but some place he shouldn’t be—perhaps the Spirit Realm of the dead?

Could it be that fish soup? Had it poisoned him so badly that Nature’s Shelter activated? Was he in the world of Nature’s Shelter right now?

Frowning, Leon couldn’t be sure. There was too little information.

“Lines? Oh, I see.” The old man, still hidden from sight, seemed to relax.

Then he smacked Leon on the forehead again, ignoring Leon’s attempt to block. The flick landed directly.

“You’re still young—don’t waste time on meaningless TV dramas, brat.”

The tone was warm yet full of frustration, and Leon froze for a moment before asking cautiously, “Old man… who are you?”

“I’m your grandpa!” The old man’s voice rang again. It almost sounded like he was teasing Leon, but Leon didn’t take it that way.

“Grandpa?! So I really did die, huh? Damned Nature’s Shelter—wasn’t it supposed to prevent me from dying, to buy me time for treatment? And now I’m just… gone?”

Leon didn’t doubt the old man’s words. If he dared to claim he was Leon’s grandfather, Leon was willing to believe it.

But that wasn’t the real point. The point was—Grandpa had passed away long ago, and if they could meet now, didn’t that mean Leon was already dead?

“Dead, my ass. You just wandered into the Spirit Realm for a bit. I don’t know why, but I can feel your soul is growing stronger. That’s a good thing. But you’re not supposed to be here yet, so… scram!”

Before Leon could react, he felt his entire body being crushed into a ball, stuffed into a cannon, and fired like a shell.

Darkness swallowed his vision. The air was icy cold, like being thrown naked onto the Siberian tundra—utterly helpless.

Just as Leon felt he might freeze solid, an invisible giant hand scooped him up. Warmth rushed back into his body, and his sight returned.

The familiar blue sky. The soft grass under his back. The scent of earth in the air. And above him—two familiar old faces: Rasmodius and Linus.

“I’m alive again?” Leon propped himself up and looked around. He was back in Stardew Valley, right by Linus’s tent. It was as if the entire experience had been nothing more than a dream.

“You never died. For some special reason, your soul just entered the Spirit Realm. So… did you see your grandpa?” Linus asked curiously.

“Didn’t see him directly. He seemed to be avoiding me. We talked, but… I forgot what he said.”

Leon rubbed his forehead, trying to recall. The memory of the conversation was fading fast. In just that short moment, the details were gone.

Seeing his dazed look, Linus and Rasmodius exchanged glances. Linus raised an eyebrow, signaling Rasmodius to check Leon’s condition.

Rasmodius shook his head while nodding slightly—Leon was fine. The forgotten conversation was probably that old man’s doing on purpose.

“Well, forget it. If it were important, you’d remember. What you need now is to go home, sleep, and rest,” Linus said, brushing the grass and dirt off Leon’s back.

“Mm.” Leon didn’t refuse. He still felt a bit woozy, and maybe lying down for a while was indeed the best choice.
