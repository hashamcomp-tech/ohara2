Chapter 138: 138 | StellarNote [CASTLE BONUS]

Jordan watched Kumiko’s fingers move across the keyboard as she downloaded OBS from the official website. The progress bar crawled at the kind of speed that made him want to throw his phone at the wall, but Kumiko seemed completely unbothered. She hummed some K-pop song under her breath and organized desktop icons with the kind of obsessive precision that probably kept her room spotless and her streaming schedule color-coded.

Alexis had left twenty minutes ago after checking her phone one too many times and announcing that charity luncheons waited for no one, not even computer builds. She’d hugged Chloe and Kumiko goodbye and given Jordan a weird little wave that managed to be both dismissive and friendly at the same time. Classic Alexis energy.

"Alright," Kumiko said as the download finished. "Time for the fun part. What’s your streaming name going to be?"

The question sat there like a test Jordan hadn’t studied for. Chloe was cross-legged on the floor wearing his old volleyball shirt and black shorts that made his brain do stupid things, looking at him like he might actually have an answer worth hearing.

"I mean, what do you want it to be?" Jordan asked, settling beside her on the carpet. "It’s your channel."

"I can’t use Calypso," Chloe said immediately, her face going pink in that way that made his chest feel tight. "That would be embarrassing if someone recognized me on stream."

Jordan laughed and rubbed the back of his head, remembering exactly how he’d recognized those blue streaks from her OnlyFans content. "Yeah, probably best to avoid that connection."

Here they were, building a legitimate streaming career to replace her adult content work, and he was the guy who’d discovered both versions of her by accident. He’d gone from paying subscriber to boyfriend in the span of two weeks, and somehow that felt both completely insane and perfectly normal.

"What name do you go by, Kumiko?"

"KumiKumoCos," Kumiko said with obvious pride. "Kumi for my name, Kumo means spider in Japanese, and Cos for cosplay. I thought it sounded cute and mysterious."

"That’s actually pretty clever," Chloe said, leaning forward to watch Kumiko navigate the OBS setup wizard. "Mine needs to be something completely different from anything I’ve used before."

Jordan’s brain started working through possibilities. The smart play would be something that reflected Chloe’s personality without giving away her background or connecting to her previous online presence. Something fresh but memorable, professional but not boring.

"What about BlueNote?" he suggested. "Like, for your hair streak and because you sing?"

"Ooh, that’s not bad," Kumiko said, pausing her clicking to look over. "It’s musical but not too obvious."

"BlueNote..." Chloe repeated the name slowly, testing how it sounded. "I like it, but what if someone else already has it?"

"We can check," Kumiko said, opening Twitch in another tab. She typed the name into the search bar and waited. "It’s taken, but BlueNoteGaming is available. So is BlueNoteLive."

"BlueNoteGaming makes it sound like I only play games," Chloe said, wrinkling her nose. "But I want to do Just Chatting streams and maybe cooking content eventually."

Jordan watched her think it through, the way she bit her lower lip when concentrating. The System had been suspiciously quiet all morning, probably because he was ignoring the seventy-two hour registration deadline ticking down like a bomb timer. Right now he just wanted to focus on helping Chloe build something that belonged to her, something that had nothing to do with supernatural phone apps or inheritance requirements.

"What about your real name mixed with something else?" Kumiko suggested. "Like ChloeBlueTunes or something?"

"That sounds like a children’s show," Chloe said with a laugh. "I need something that doesn’t sound like it was made by a mom trying to be hip."

"Fair point." Jordan leaned back on his hands, studying Chloe’s face as she scrolled through Twitch streamers for inspiration. "What matters to you? Like, what do you want people to think when they hear your name?"

The question made Chloe pause, her finger hovering over the mouse. "I want them to think I’m real. Not fake, not trying too hard. Just... genuine."

Something twisted in Jordan’s chest at that word. Here she was, trying to build an authentic online presence, and he was sitting next to her with a magical phone app grading their relationship like a dating sim. The contrast made him feel like a fraud wearing his own face.

"What about something Korean?" Kumiko suggested. "Like NalGae? It means wings in Korean."

"That’s pretty," Chloe said, but she didn’t sound convinced. "I don’t know if I want to make my ethnicity the main focus, though. I want the content to speak for itself."

Jordan understood that instinct. In a world where people got reduced to single characteristics, Chloe wanted to be known for her personality and talents first, not categorized by demographics. Smart thinking for building a sustainable streaming career.

"Okay, different approach," Jordan said. "What’s something you love that isn’t obvious? Like, something people wouldn’t expect?"

Chloe tilted her head, considering. "I really love old movies. Like, black and white classics. Casablanca, Roman Holiday, Singin’ in the Rain. My dad and I used to watch them together on Sunday afternoons."

"What about something movie-related then?" Kumiko said gently. "Like VintageVibe or ClassicChlo?"

"ClassicChlo sounds like a discount clothing store," Chloe said, but she was smiling. "VintageVibe is cute, though."

Jordan watched Kumiko check the availability while his brain turned over other possibilities. Movie references, vintage themes, something that captured Chloe’s aesthetic without being too literal.

"Available!" Kumiko announced. "VintageVibe is totally free."

"Wait," Jordan said as an idea hit him. "What about something with ’stellar’? Like, old Hollywood had the star system, and you’re going to be a star."

Both girls turned to look at him, and Jordan felt his face heat up under their attention. "I mean, if that’s not too cheesy."

"StellarVibe?" Chloe tested the name. "Actually, that’s not terrible."

"StellarChlo?" Kumiko suggested.

"StellarNote?" Jordan added, thinking of the music angle.

"Ooh, I like StellarNote," Chloe said, sitting up straighter. "It’s got the star thing, the music thing, and it doesn’t sound like I’m trying too hard."

Kumiko typed it into Twitch search and grinned. "Available! StellarNote it is."
