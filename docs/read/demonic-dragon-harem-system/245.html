The message was clear about moving forward, but… Strax was thinking about something, and his response…

"I don't want to." He suddenly said, making not only Scarlet freeze but also Albert, who looked at him with an expression Strax had never seen in his entire life.

"W-What?" Scarlet stammered for a second.

"You heard me. I couldn't care less," Strax said, pointing at her. "Despite you being a hell of a beauty that I'd love to make my wife for the rest of my life, you're more dangerous than my father—and mind you, my father's supposedly the strongest in the Human Realm," he said rationally and… quite honestly.

'Well, I tried to keep him alive, but it's your fault for wanting Scarlet to train him. Sorry, dear,' Albert thought.

"Honestly, I really am interested in you. You seem stronger than that idiot I have to call 'Dad,' so I'd have no reason to say no. But I still have my wives, and even though I'm interested, I wouldn't just leave them to 'train' with you, Miss Primordial Vampire and blah blah blah." Strax said casually, even mocking the woman, who fell silent… How could she even respond to something like that? She'd never encountered someone so honest before…

Naturally, she would kill those who desired her body. In fact, she always killed anyone who looked at her with lust. But this man? He wasn't being lecherous despite calling her a "hell of a beauty I'd love to make my wife."

"How many wives do you have?" she asked directly, curious.

"Officially, four." Strax said, "Unofficially, add four more," he added. "Including spirits, another four." He finished.

"You're obsessed with the number four, aren't you?" Scarlet questioned. "Yes, four's a good number," he replied.

"Well, I'm going to kidnap him. Is that fine with you?" Scarlet asked, looking at Albert. "Do as you like. He asked for it," Albert replied, and Strax froze. "W-What do you—"

"I've already decided, boy." Suddenly, Scarlet vanished and reappeared in front of him, and with a simple gesture, she struck Strax's stomach with immense force.

"Cough!" He lost his breath from the attack, and another blow to his neck quickly knocked him unconscious. It all happened so fast.

"Oh…~ He just had to accept…" Albert muttered, rubbing his temple. "That boy really loves to dance on the edge of life and death…" he said, watching the red-haired woman sling him over her shoulder like a sack of potatoes.

"What about this idiot's women?" Albert asked, noticing Scarlet's face looked… odd.

"I'm taking him to Nightingale. I'd already warned that idiot king I'd be bringing someone. So just give them this." Scarlet tossed him a red seal. "The Vermillion Clan grants permission to humans carrying this," she said, turning around.

"Our business is done, White Tiger," she said, heading for the exit. "Don't kill him," Albert said, and she glanced back at him.

"I'd say the same to you, considering how terrible a father you've been to him. Or do you think I don't know about leaving him trapped in a mine?" Scarlet replied, her blood-red eyes glowing, almost piercing him through.

"That woman would've hated you if she knew how much you let the son she loved suffer." She said before disappearing suddenly, as if teleporting.

Albert stood still, staring at the empty space where Scarlet had vanished with Strax.

Her words still echoed in his mind, each like a blade cutting deep.

He felt a certain guilt for having to hear something like that—especially from a woman like her. Unlike others, he would have ignored it; after all, no one had the power to oppose him… except that woman. The only woman he respected, not because he liked her, but because of her strength.

"Damn bloodsucker," Albert muttered, clutching the red seal Scarlet had left behind. He let out a heavy sigh, massaging his temples as his head throbbed with pain. "Screw it," he said.
