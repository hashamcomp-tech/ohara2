Chapter 174

"Did you hear that?" Evelyn asked after Strax and Samira had left her 'House'. She still couldn't quite believe what he had mentioned, even though she had agreed to help by lending her 'strength'—if that's what her hired mercenaries could be called.

"He's either a genius… or a complete idiot…" Lyana said, having remained hidden the entire time, just listening to what that mischievous man was planning. Of course, she wasn't doing it for herself; it was because Evelyn had asked her to stay hidden and only intervene if he tried to harm her or if her physical safety was at risk.

Even though Evelyn was more willing than ever, after so much time suffering in solitude, it was still hard to trust a man with such strange and illogical ideas… That's why Evelyn needed direction, a personal opinion from someone she trusted… fortunately, this time…

"I agree." She said, how could she not? What that man wanted to do was illogical…

Just think about it! He didn't even try to hide it. When she commented about the city being destroyed, he quickly changed his plans to dominate the city. It was so obvious that he wasn't concerned with the real plans anymore that she couldn't help but agree with him—it was so absurd, so idiotic that…

"He's going to succeed." Lyana said, firmly admitting that it was so ridiculous that… "I know, he's going to succeed." Evelyn repeated, not out of trust in him, but because she believed in his sheer audacity and the absurdity of his plan…

"I can't believe I'm doing this, you know?" Lyana said as she sat in the same spot where Strax had been not long ago.

"Neither can I." Evelyn admitted.

"Ah…" The two sighed together as they tried not to let their frustrations spill out onto the table…

"At least, does that remedy really exist? I mean, we need to be sure about that…" Lyana questioned, but Evelyn shook her head, "It exists… at least he firmly believes it does, especially since I couldn't detect any lies in his words… Despite… him being extremely honest… it's almost frightening." She said.

Lyana pondered for a few seconds on what she could do to avoid thinking about bad things coming from Strax, since he was still trying to help her Mistress despite the obvious issues between them. Lyana and Strax might have problems, but her mistress didn't hold any grudges or animosities… She was even too calm, and that irritated Lyana a bit.

"So, should I prepare?" Lyana asked, seeing that, even knowing this, she couldn't change her Mistress's decision. "Of course." Evelyn said with a smile, but before that…

"Just one question… Why did you agree to this when we could just buy the item?" Lyana questioned. It was really strange for her to have agreed to something so surreal…

"Hm? Of course, it's for fun." Evelyn said with a smile. "W-What?" Lyana asked, unable to comprehend what her Mistress was thinking… This was… idiotic?

"Hey, don't look at me like that, okay? I just thought it would be fun to take part in a big heist," she said with a grin. "You're starting to resemble him." Lyana said.

"Who?" Evelyn questioned.

"You know exactly who I'm talking about, that lunatic who just left here with a plan to create a new force in the city and dominate the damn black market!" Lyana shouted, completely losing her composure…

Well… we all knew she would…

"Hey, calm down, okay? We've already agreed that everything will work out, and we owe him, so what's the harm in having some fun?" Evelyn said playfully, while Lyana's worry only grew. She couldn't quantify how nervous this situation was making her…

"Seriously… I need a new mistress." Lyana murmured nervously, trying not to unload another wave of negative thoughts onto her. Fortunately, she managed to keep her composure… for now…

"So, what do we do?" she asked. "We wait until the day of the auction. Prepare as best you can; we'll probably join forces with that Rogue he mentioned. Also… try to buy information about anyone named Strax in the black market… This guy is still hiding something, something I have no idea about… Strax Antares doesn't really fit." Evelyn said.

"We already know he's lying about the name, so what's the point of finding out? What if Strax is also a false name?" Lyana said, but Evelyn shook her head, "The way his wives call him, I've noticed that… They're not skilled enough liars to fake something like that. His name is Strax… I can confirm that…"

Lyana silently listened to what her mistress said, but she had her own suspicions… 'A man this strong, coming from Vorah… without any etiquette like a knight, but able to wield not one, but two spiritual swords of special grade… well, to me, it seems obvious.' She thought, but didn't voice anything to her Mistress; it was still too absurd to consider, despite all the evidence…

"Alright, we'll keep an eye on him," Lyana said as she stood up, "I'll do as you command…" she said, disappearing like a shadow.

"I just hope I don't regret this… Maybe my time is shorter than I think…" Evelyn murmured, looking at her hands, which were visibly losing color as the veins turned blue…

Evelyn looked at her hands, the faded color of her skin reflecting the deterioration she tried to hide from everyone, even herself. Strax's promise was too tempting, and the opportunity to participate in something as grand as taking over a city — even just for fun — offered her a powerful distraction from the slow advance of the disease that was consuming her.

"Fun, but also risky..." she murmured to herself, her thoughts echoing in the empty room. "But maybe... this is exactly what I need. A little chaos before the end."

She got up from the chair, slowly walking to the window of her office. Outside, the city pulsed with life, completely unaware of what was being planned in the shadows. Evelyn felt a chill as she thought about what was to come. The idea of dismantling the entire local economy and building something new from the ashes was terrifying, but also exciting.

"And if he actually succeeds...?" Evelyn wondered, doubt mixed with a hint of hope. Perhaps, just perhaps, Strax's insane plan was the key to everything. Her cure, the power she so desired... all of it seemed within reach, as long as she played her cards right.

But the question that still lingered in her mind was: who really was Strax? She knew there was more to him than he let on. A man who mastered special-grade spiritual swords, coming from an unknown land, with such unbridled ambition... He was an enigma, and Evelyn didn't like enigmas she couldn't decipher.

"Lyana is right to be suspicious. We need to find out more about him before it's too late," she murmured, her hands unconsciously clenching into fists.

With one last sigh, Evelyn stepped away from the window, feeling the weight of her decisions on her shoulders. She knew that, no matter what happened, there was no turning back. She was too deeply involved, both in her search for a cure and in the game Strax had proposed.

The auction was approaching, and with it, the beginning of something that could change the fate of many. Evelyn just hoped that, in the end, she wasn't trading her soul for a little more time... or fun.

As she prepared for what was to come...
