Chapter 308

The vampires around watched in absolute silence as Scarlet and Elizabeth took their positions in the makeshift arena. The tension in the air was almost palpable, and everyone’s gaze was fixed on the two figures, one facing the other. Elizabeth was the first to move, adjusting her stance with elegant grace, but with fierce determination in her eyes.

Scarlet, on the other hand, seemed completely at ease. Her casual and relaxed smile indicated that she wasn’t taking the duel too seriously. Her arms were crossed, and she seemed more interested in analyzing Elizabeth’s movements than preparing for an immediate attack.

"Come on, princess," Scarlet taunted, tilting her head slightly. "Show me what you’ve got. I promise I’ll try not to get too bored."

Elizabeth gritted her teeth, channeling her energy with precision. A cold aura began radiating from her body, freezing the ground around her instantly. Without hesitation, she advanced, firing several ice blades toward Scarlet with impressive speed. The blades sliced through the air with a deadly whistle, but Scarlet remained motionless.

An Ice Elemental... what a joke... Scarlet thought.

As the first projectile was about to hit her, Scarlet simply sidestepped, her movement so fluid it looked like a dance. Each ice blade passed by just grazing her, not even scratching her skin. The onlookers murmured in admiration, but Elizabeth didn’t lose focus.

"Hmph. Not a bad start," Scarlet commented, still wearing that annoying smile. "But if this is what you consider ’proving your strength,’ you might still be far off."

Elizabeth narrowed her eyes, her frustration beginning to grow. Without wasting any time, she clapped her hands together, summoning a massive ice pillar that shot up from the ground, attempting to catch Scarlet by surprise. The structure rose quickly, but Scarlet leapt backward with almost careless lightness, landing several meters away with no effort whatsoever.

"Interesting. A bit more strength this time," Scarlet admitted, her eyes shimmering with excitement. "Maybe you’re not entirely mediocre."

However, Elizabeth didn’t stop there. Taking advantage of the opening, she conjured several ice spears, each glowing with an intense blue light, and hurled them toward Scarlet with deadly precision. This time, Scarlet raised an eyebrow, finally lifting her hand. A simple snap of her fingers was enough to deflect all the spears, which fell to the ground, shattering into glittering crystals.

"Are you testing me, or are you actually trying to hit me, Elizabeth?" Scarlet asked, her voice laced with sarcasm.

Focusing herself, she channeled even more energy, summoning a massive snowstorm around the arena. The winds howled, and visibility drastically decreased, but Scarlet stood still, as though nothing had changed.

"Interesting..." Scarlet murmured to herself. "Well, if that’s how you want to play, I guess I can try a little harder."

When Elizabeth emerged from the storm, her ice sword already summoned and ready to strike, Scarlet finally moved to retaliate. With a swift motion, she raised her hand and blocked the blade with just two fingers. The shockwave caused the ground to tremble, but Scarlet maintained her calm expression.

"Is that all?" she asked, tilting her head to the side. "If it is, I guess I’ll have to teach you a lesson or two about what true strength means."

Strax stood in a corner of the circle, watching with a lazy smile on his face. He twirled the blood-filled chalice in his hand, his eyes glowing with interest as he analyzed every movement of Scarlet and Elizabeth.

"Elizabeth has guts," he muttered to himself. "But Scarlet… she’s barely started. This is going to be fun."

Daniela, who was standing beside Strax, snorted. "Mom’s going to play with her until she loses interest. I bet she’ll end this in a few minutes."

Cassandra smiled slightly, her eyes fixed on the fight. "Mom won’t finish so quickly. She likes this. She enjoys testing others, even if the outcome is inevitable. Elizabeth has no idea what she’s gotten into..."

Shit... Elizabeth stepped back, frustrated. She knew Scarlet was toying with her, but she couldn’t let that affect her determination. Channeling more energy, she created an explosion of ice that covered the field in seconds. The ground froze completely, and sharp icicles shot up around Scarlet, trying to trap her.

Scarlet looked at the ice surrounding her with curiosity, clicking her tongue. "A trap? How cute."

With a simple gesture of her hand, Scarlet’s aura exploded, shattering all the ice into tiny pieces. Elizabeth was forced to shield herself from the rain of fragments, her breath becoming heavier. Scarlet, however, seemed untouched, as if she hadn’t exerted any effort at all.

"Enough of the warm-up," Scarlet said, finally adopting a more offensive stance. "Let’s see how much you can really endure."

With impressive speed, Scarlet disappeared from her position, reappearing behind Elizabeth in the blink of an eye. Before Elizabeth could react, Scarlet struck the air, creating a shockwave that sent Elizabeth flying several meters backward. The vampire princess landed with difficulty, but rose to her feet, her determination still burning in her eyes.

Strax grinned wider, taking a sip from his chalice. "Here she goes. Scarlet can’t resist playing with her prey."

Belatrix rolled her eyes. "Elizabeth should give up now. She has no chance, and everyone here knows it."

"Maybe she’s not fighting to win," Cassandra suggested calmly. "Maybe she’s trying to prove something to herself." She shrugged.

Daniela snorted again. "Prove she can get beaten by Scarlet? What an accomplishment."

Strax merely laughed, not taking his eyes off the field. "You underestimate the importance of a battle like this. Elizabeth is learning more than it seems. But Scarlet… well, she’s just having fun..."

When the smoke cleared, Scarlet stood in the same spot, still looking at Elizabeth with a smile that was even more dangerous.

"That was a good effort," Scarlet admitted, tilting her head. "But you’re still light-years away from catching up to me." She smiled...
