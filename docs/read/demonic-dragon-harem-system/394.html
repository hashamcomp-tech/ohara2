Chapter 394

The tension in the air had become palpable, as if every breath was a spark waiting to ignite a cataclysm. The thunder of Zeus echoed in the distance, like a war drum heralding the imminent clash between gods who had upheld only a fragile, silent truce for ages.

Nyx walked slowly toward Zeus, wrapped in a penumbra so dense the ground beneath her feet seemed to dissolve into liquid darkness. Her gaze was serene, but her eyes—luminous slits within the veil—burned with ancient rage.

"Your war must be going worse than I thought," she said with a millennia-old chill. "To invade the world itself… Do you truly understand what that means?"

Zeus didn't move, but his aura crackled with renewed force.

"What it means, Nyx," he replied, his voice laced with the arrogance of a thousand victories, "is that your dominion of shadows no longer inspires fear. You are no longer the primordial terror you once were at the dawn of time."

"Oh? So now you wield insolence?" Nyx raised an eyebrow, like someone watching a child play with a dagger. "Just because your pantheon thinks itself above the others, you believe you can trample over the rules? The barriers between realms weren't built on a whim. They were made to contain horrors even you wouldn't dare name."

"The world is dying!" Zeus roared, and the sky split open with a crackling bolt of lightning that tore through the clouds and made the earth tremble. "You, the Abyss, the Ancients... you all sit in silence while the Veil crumbles! I was the only one who acted!"

Xenovia, removed from the divine quarrel, knelt beside Strax, who was gasping as the light of his transformation slowly faded. His armor flaked away in scaly shards, revealing scorched, mana-burned skin that was healing through Nyx's residual energy.

"Strax..." Xenovia whispered, touching his face gently. "Are you okay?"

"I'm fine... just need a moment," he muttered, rising slowly. "Been worse, I think."

Xenovia let out a soft laugh, relief softening her tear-filled eyes.

At that moment, Scarlet appeared beside him, arms crossed, expression tight. The rain began to fall in thin drops, merging with the steam still rising from the shattered battlefield.

"Idiot," she said, staring at him intensely. "You could've died. You could've lost control, destroyed everything and—" Her voice trembled with restrained emotion.

Strax turned his head toward her. "You know me... When everything's on the brink of collapse, that's when I decide to do something incredibly stupid."

Scarlet sighed, stepping closer. Her fingers brushed through his sweat-dampened hair with a tender restraint. "Yeah… and that's exactly why I keep loving you, you reckless bastard."

Meanwhile, Nyx and Zeus now stood face to face, only a few feet apart, separated by an invisible line of power. The sky above was half-starred, half-storm.

"You've always thought yourself above the rules," Nyx said, her voice softer now, but heavy enough to make the air vibrate. "But invading this plane? That isn't just reckless, Zeus. It's an act of war."

"The war is already here," he replied coldly. "So let it come. You hid for eons, and now you play judge of fate."

"You Olympians are still as arrogant as ever," Nyx murmured, her tone laced with a patience stretched to its limit. The shadows around her pulsed with ancient disdain.

Strax remained lying down, but his eyes were open—keen, alert. The voices of gods echoed throughout the ruined throne—Zeus's thunder and Nyx's whispers of darkness seemed to erode the very notion of time.

Suddenly, he laughed. A dry, low, bitter sound.

"What a joke."Silence fell like a blade. Everyone turned toward him.

"The great and powerful gods..." Strax staggered forward. "Started a war amongst themselves. And for what? Let me guess..."

He looked at Nyx, then Zeus, his eyes burning like embers.

"Split into two little teams like spoiled children. Now you're trying to wipe each other out, and the winner gets to decide the fate of the world? That it? That's the grand design of the divine beings who think themselves above everything?"

"Strax..." Scarlet murmured cautiously, stepping beside him. "Not now..."

But he didn't hear her. He couldn't.

"This world already bleeds because of you. And now you dare come here... into my Realm..."His voice deepened, resonating through the stone walls like the earth itself was trembling at each syllable.

"Get the fuck out of my Realm."

The air shattered like glass.The ground shook.A wave of raw energy burst from Strax's body, flinging dust and debris in every direction.

In seconds, he began to grow.

Muscles warped. Bones stretched. Wings erupted with a thunderclap, tearing the air. His skin shifted into runic scales that shimmered between obsidian and crimson.

With a roar that silenced thunder and made even Nyx take a step back, the ceiling of the throne hall exploded in a storm of stone and arcane force.

And then, rising above all — majestic, colossal, serpentine, and terrifying — stood the true Strax.

A gargantuan serpent-dragon, crowned with ethereal fire, with eyes that burned like black suns.His tail coiled and shattered pillars as his wings, vast and alive with ancient power, blotted out the sky above the ruined throne hall.

He lowered his head, eyes locked on the gods.

"You may play your divine war in your rotting realms. You may devour each other like starving dogs chasing power..."

Strax's voice echoed like an ancient storm, older than time itself.Each word thundered with the weight of a thousand roars, shaking the earth and stirring flames into a wild dance.

"...but this place — this is not your battlefield."

The serpentine dragon rose even higher, his presence eclipsing the stars themselves.His gaze burned like furnaces, and even the heavens above seemed to hesitate before him.

"If you want to continue this divine farce..." he growled, baring fangs capable of devouring worlds, "...then you'll have to go through me first."

A suffocating silence engulfed the throne hall.

Zeus clenched his fists. Lightning began to crackle around his form, twisting into serpents of pure celestial fury.The power radiating from the King of Olympus was crushing — overwhelming.But he didn't move. Not yet.

His eyes were locked on the creature before him. It wasn't fear… but respect — or perhaps bitter rage, the kind born from recognizing a worthy adversary.

"A dragon..." Zeus murmured, as if trying to make sense of something that didn't belong in his neatly ordered universe.His golden eyes flickered with distrust."But not one from Caelum..."

The air trembled with power, tension, and dread.The shattered walls of the throne hall quivered at every word exchanged between Nyx and Zeus, as the sky above raged with divine fury.

At the center of it all, Strax's massive form loomed like a forgotten god — serpentine, unyielding, eternal.And he wasn't willing to listen anymore.

"I'm done with this bullshit."

The voice came deep, guttural, echoing through the walls with the weight of a final sentence.Before anyone could react, Strax lowered his head toward Grunnar — the vessel of Hades — who lay wounded among the rubble.

A moment of silence.

Then, hell itself.

A jet of dense blue fire, roaring like a hurricane, burst from the dragon's throat.The breath struck Grunnar like a divine spear, piercing through flesh, bone, and soul.The warrior didn't even have time to scream — he was disintegrated, engulfed in ethereal flames that consumed him in a single second.

Silence returned. Heavier now.

Then, an invisible notification swept through the space.

[You have slain the vessel of Ares.]

The gods froze.

Zeus stepped forward, eyes sparking, but he didn't dare attack.

Nyx stood still, her dark gaze locked onto Strax's form.

High above, the dragon slowly turned his head.His eyes fell on Kaelis, the vessel of Athena.She swallowed hard, her body tensing as if she could already feel the shadow of death.

"Scarlet," Strax said, his voice low but filled with absolute power.

Scarlet understood instantly.She moved like a shadow, appearing before Kaelis in the blink of an eye.

Kaelis's eyes widened. "No—"

But Scarlet had already wrapped her hand around the woman's throat.

"You should've chosen another side," she whispered, cold as steel.

With a sharp snap, Kaelis's neck was twisted with brutal force.Her head fell, eyes still wide open, rolling across the blood-stained rubble.

[Vessel of Athena has been killed by your wife.]

Now the silence was total.

Xenovia, further back, raised a trembling hand to her mouth, shocked.Nyx arched an eyebrow.Zeus clenched his teeth so hard that sparks cracked from his divine aura.

"You've... gone mad," he said, his voice echoing like a restrained thunderclap.

But Strax didn't answer.His gaze remained fixed, his expression unmoved.He had made his choice.This was no longer a divine debate.It was a warning.

Two vessels — sacred pieces in a game older than memory — had been exterminated in seconds.Not by ritual. Not through lengthy war.But by will.By decision.

Strax had made his stance clear.And now they all understood:He wasn't just another dragon.Not a pawn. Not a mortal elevated.

He was a king.

A sovereign who would not bow — not even to the heavens.

Scarlet returned to his side, Kaelis's body behind her already fading, the connection to Athena unraveling like mist in the morning light.

"Anyone else want to continue?" Strax asked.His voice was softer now — but infinitely more terrifying.

No one answered.
