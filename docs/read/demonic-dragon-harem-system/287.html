Daniela, Belatrix, and Cassandra, the powerful daughters of Scarlet, knelt on the ground, their postures rigid and tense, while Scarlet, with a mischievous grin, stood before them, wearing nothing but a towel that barely covered the essentials, her arms crossed with an expression of disapproval.

"You've gotten quite naughty... spying on your own mother?" Scarlet said, her voice dripping with ironic amusement.

"Mom... we didn't... we didn't mean to... Oh, shit." Daniela began, clearly at a loss for words, her nervous expression struggling to come up with a plausible excuse. She knew there was no way out of the scolding, but her pride wouldn't allow her to admit the mistake outright.

It's not like it was a big secret, but... well, curiosity is hard to control, isn't it?" she said, attempting to offer a rational justification, but her tone was clearly nervous.

Belatrix, on the other hand, was visibly uncomfortable, her face flushed and unsure of where to look.

"We just... thought, you know... something was wrong. We needed to see with our own eyes," she added, her voice trembling, clearly without a convincing argument.

Scarlet observed each of her daughters with a malicious smile, clearly enjoying their discomfort. She crossed her arms more firmly, leaning slightly against the doorframe.

"I didn't know my daughters were so... bold," she continued in a teasing tone, her eyes glinting with a subtle challenge. "Spying on their own mother? Tsk, tsk... I think I'll have to teach you some respect."

Daniela, unable to maintain her composure any longer, let out an irritated sigh.

"Okay, okay! We shouldn't have done it. We get it, no need to make a drama out of it," she grumbled, but her gaze betrayed a mixture of discomfort and a hint of amusement.

The three sisters looked at her with a mix of apprehension and confusion, unsure whether she was serious or just joking.

Scarlet, now with a more serious expression, took a step forward, looking more imposing than ever despite the towel still not covering everything completely. She gazed at them with an enigmatic smile, her eyes glinting with a touch of malice.

"You'll learn what happens when someone tries to control or challenge me," she said, offering no further explanation.

Belatrix, now more alert, looked at Scarlet with a mix of curiosity and a bit of fear. "And how are you going to do that, Mom?" she asked, clearly trying to stay calm, although it was evident that she wasn't entirely comfortable with the situation.

Scarlet merely let out a low laugh, a soft sound, but full of intent.

"You'll have to figure that out for yourselves, darlings. Let's start with a little training."

Daniela's eyes widened, and the other sisters tensed, not knowing what to expect as Scarlet moved away from the door, suggesting that the "lesson" was about to begin. The air in the room grew heavy with tension, but also with a sense of fun and anticipation—something that, for her daughters, was a disorienting mix of emotions.

Scarlet then looked back at the three of them, a mischievous gleam in her eyes. "Now... who's going to be the first to learn the lesson?"
