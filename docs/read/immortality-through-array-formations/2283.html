Chapter 2283: Chapter 1053: Great Wilderness Order (Part 3)

He could only send it randomly to a group.

If he sent it to the wrong person, Mr. Tu’s minions might notice it immediately, and the plan would be exposed.

What to do...

Mo Hua’s gaze sharpened, and suddenly his heart was moved.

"Give it a try..."

At this moment, it was all about taking a risk and having a bit of "tacit understanding."

Mo Hua manipulated the magnetic patterns through the Thunder Magnetism puppet, inputting commands into the Great Wilderness Order, forming the text:

"Gu Changhuai has committed numerous evil deeds and is a great concern to my master."

"Kill Venerable Fire Buddha, destroy the Bi Mountain Holy Hall, slay Shui Yanluo, blow up the Rouge Boat, wreak havoc at the Dragon King Temple, his evil deeds are countless and deserve to be executed."

"Now that the Large Formation is complete, followers, heed the order, spare no cost to kill Gu Changhuai, no mistakes allowed!"

...

At times like this, both sides are certainly thinking about killing each other.

To kill, you must naturally kill the "greatest threat."

And Uncle Gu obviously made it onto the Evil God’s "must-kill" list.

This message, mixed in with jumbled information, would not seem out of place even if noticed.

After Mo Hua finished writing, he directly "group sent" it.

Many of the Evil God’s minions, monster cultivators, evil cultivators, and demon cultivators received this message.

Among them, some evil demon cultivators had originally received orders to kill Gu Changhuai, so receiving this message was normal.

Furthermore, the vast majority of demon cultivators, being brutal and bloodthirsty, glanced at it and didn’t care.

After all, they only wanted to kill people and didn’t care at all if the one killed was Gu Changhuai or not.

But while they didn’t care about this message, some could not ignore it.

Outside Yanluo Mountain, in a certain camp.

A group of Taoist Court officials gathered, looking at the overwhelming blood-red barrier in the distance, and the astonishing Large Formation, feeling anxious.

The top Heavenly Pride disciples of the Qianxue Four Sects and Eight Gates-twelve streams were trapped in the Large Formation.

These proud students were connected to the great families of Qianxue, making the matter extremely significant.

If these disciples could be saved, at least the Taoist Court could give an account of the situation.

But if they couldn’t be saved and all these Heavenly Prides died in the Large Formation, it would undoubtedly cause an earth-shattering disaster.

This responsibility would fall on the Taoist Court, whether they wanted it or not.

If the various forces held them accountable, these officials might all lose their positions.

Moreover, many who held positions in the Taoist Court were from aristocratic families.

Among those trapped Heavenly Pride disciples, there were their juniors or nephews, carrying the family’s expectations and future, now on the brink of life or death; how could they not be anxious.

But the Evil Path Great Formation before them was truly powerful and bizarre.

The Taoist Court, along with various family sects, attacked for many days but could not find a breakthrough, instead losing many people.

The blood-red Large Formation over Yanluo Mountain was like a bloody "giant beast," settled in the mountain, continuously consuming life, absorbing blood Qi, and growing stronger, not giving them any opportunity to strike.

And within the Large Formation, all news was completely isolated.

They still didn’t know whether the sect Heavenly Prides inside were alive or dead.

Even "death" was not the worst.

Once polluted by evil Qi, falling into the Demon Path, it would bring shame to the family and sect, and their exceptional talents might even be exploited by the Demon Path, growing into a ruthless, infamous Demon Path leader in the future...

This was something nobody wanted to see.

The Taoist Court officials felt a huge stone weighing on their hearts, making it hard for them to breathe.

All they could do was seize the time and spare no expense in preparing the next attack.

Hoping that through continuous attacks, they could find a breakthrough in the Large Formation.

As the officials were discussing, a tactical leader quietly walked up to Gu Changhuai and handed him a roughly made, unmarked token, softly reminding him:

"Supervisor Gu, be cautious, someone seems intent on killing you..."

Gu Changhuai frowned, "Who?"

The tactical leader pointed at the token, "See for yourself..."

Gu Changhuai took the token, glanced over it, and didn’t pay much attention.

As a Taoist Court Supervisor, his job involved offending people, arresting, and killing many, with many monster and evil cultivators wanting his life.

He had seen countless similar messages over the past days.

Gu Changhuai handed the token back, instructing, "Keep an eye on these tokens, and notify me immediately if there are any abnormal messages."

Having become acquainted with Mo Hua, Gu Changhuai was also sensitive to "Letter Tokens," influenced by Mo Hua.

And now that Mo Hua was trapped in the Large Formation, life or death unknown, though Gu Changhuai didn’t express it, he was secretly anxious.

Thinking that Mo Hua was sharp and versatile, especially skilled in Yuan Magnetic Communication, perhaps able to send some messages out through Letter Tokens; hence, any war spoils that could transmit letters, Gu Changhuai would collect and order to be closely monitored, notifying him of any unusual signs immediately.

Unfortunately, up to now, Gu Changhuai had not received any communication from Mo Hua.

Some new officers, even those dispatched from the Taoist Court, felt that Gu Changhuai was somewhat "daydreaming."

Trapped inside the Evil Path Great Formation, cut off from the outside, how could a Foundation Establishment disciple possibly transmit messages?

But Gu Changhuai was unwilling to give up, his mind occupied only by Mo Hua.

The messenger reticule approached with a slight bow and was ready to withdraw.

As he turned, in a flash of inspiration, Gu Changhuai suddenly called out in surprise, "Wait a second."

The messenger’s expression was puzzled, "Supervisor Gu..."

Gu Changhuai said, "Let me see that token again."

The messenger respectfully handed the token back to Gu Changhuai.

Gu Changhuai took the token, carefully reading it word by word, and gasped in realization, muttering:

"Mo Hua..."

The other officials preparing for battle looked over at Gu Changhuai with some surprise.

The elegant Xia Canonical Official asked Gu Changhuai, "What’s the matter?"

Gu Changhuai, stunned and somewhat disbelieving, replied, "This message... was sent by Mo Hua."

At this, everyone’s expressions turned to shock.

Xia Canonical Official also glanced at the text on the token, not finding it unusual, and frowned, "Are you sure?"

Gu Changhuai nodded.

To kill Venerable Fire Buddha, destroy the Bi Mountain Holy Hall, kill the Water Yama, blow up the Rouge Boat, wreak havoc at the Dragon King Temple...

These things, on the surface, did seem related to Gu Changhuai.

But thinking more deeply, many were actually done by Mo Hua.

For example, the "killing of Fire Buddha" incident—Gu Changhuai knew that the public version of the story was incorrect; the real fact was that Mo Hua pierced Fire Buddha’s heart with a chilling fireball spell.

It meant that the true killer of Fire Buddha was Mo Hua.

As for "blowing up the Rouge Boat."

The Rouge Boat was destroyed by a formation; these formations also came from Mo Hua...

These events, only a handful of those personally involved knew the truth.

Of those who knew all the details, there were only two people.

One was himself, the Taoist Court official taking the blame.

The other was Mo Hua, the "instigator."

This meant that all the intricate details regarding this message, besides himself, were known only to Mo Hua.

The message that spared no cost to "kill him" had to be sent by Mo Hua.
