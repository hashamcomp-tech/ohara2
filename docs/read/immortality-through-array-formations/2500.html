Chapter 2500: Chapter 20: Toy_2

The mist laid by experts naturally has its peculiar and profound qualities.

The unfathomably mysterious mist can cause those who probe the causality to feel confused, confusion leads to desolation, desolation to despair, and despair to a death wish.

When one harbors a death wish, self-destruction follows.

This is a bloodless method of killing.

Upon realizing this, Mr. Witch felt a surge of fear within, contemplating retreat, yet the thought of his "opportunity" lingered relentlessly in his mind.

"Since it’s a great opportunity, how could one obtain it easily without experiencing some danger?"

"However, I need to think of a way to prevent killing myself in a daze..."

Mr. Witch made up his mind, his gaze filled with greed.

...

In the luxurious guest room.

Mo Hua was resting his chin in his hand, contemplating deeply.

"Should be... still can’t kill..."

"After all, I’ve become rusty..."

Using evil qi to infuse wily minds and planting demons into causality was a Gui Tao method he had only recently researched. It had only been used once so far, so his proficiency was naturally lacking, making it unrealistic to complete a "counterattack" in one go.

"However, temporarily not being able to kill him is also fine."

Mo Hua thought to himself, "If he were truly killed, it would inevitably alert the enemy, causing unnecessary commotion."

"Moreover, this person is a rare ’toy’ that allows me to experiment with planting demons in causality and examining wily minds."

"After all, as long as a demon is planted in his Taoist Heart, killing him is just a matter of a thought..."

Upon thinking this, Mo Hua suddenly froze, wondering:

"Why do I feel... so wicked..."

Why was he thinking of treating others as "toys"?

Mo Hua frowned, reflecting for a moment before reassuring himself:

"It must be the evil qi, it’s affecting my mind, I wasn’t this bad originally..."

Mo Hua nodded in agreement.

Since his mind was influenced by the evil qi, there was nothing he could do.

He exhaled slightly, then continued to consider the matters of the Canglang Sect.

It seemed that there were still many secrets about the Canglang Sect, but upon calculating, Mo Hua realized he had already spent quite some time in the Canglang Sect.

If he continued with such "gentle" methods, who knows how much more time would be wasted.

He needed to focus on Core Formation, and time was precious.

Also, the situation in the Great Wilderness was still uncertain.

"It’s time for a swift and decisive action..."

"Should I just slaughter the Canglang Sect?"

This thought flashed through Mo Hua’s mind for an instant, but he quickly shook his head, suppressing the killing intent within.

"It’s the evil qi, it’s affecting my mind," Mo Hua comforted himself.

After calming down somewhat, Mo Hua continued reflecting:

"Definitely cannot kill the entire Canglang Sect..."

"Although most cultivators in the Canglang Sect have certainly done unscrupulous things, one cannot be sure there aren’t some with good intentions."

"Killing the wrong people would have rather serious consequences."

"Moreover, the Canglang Sect is at least a Third Grade sect; if it were truly annihilated, the Taoist Court would investigate, causing significant trouble."

"Furthermore, the Canglang Sect does have a solid foundation, not entirely useless..."

"Ideally, it would be best to kill with a borrowed knife..."

"So... who can I use to kill whom?"

Mo Hua’s eyes narrowed slightly, his gaze deep, appearing somewhat dangerous...

...

The following days passed peacefully.

Elder Ji continued sending women to Mo Hua, a different one each night, knocking on his door.

Mo Hua pretended not to see.

His thoughts were occupied with toying with his "plaything."

Originally, he thought this person, who had attempted to probe his causality and was once implanted with a wily mind, would be instilled with fear after a failed suicide attempt. Instead, it fueled his greed, making him more resolute and fearless.

Additionally, whatever means he used, though implanted with a demon in his Taoist Heart, he amazingly didn’t "kill" himself.

Mo Hua admired his courage and acknowledged his capability.

As a result, this "toy" could be enjoyed a while longer.

Mo Hua could use him as a "medium" to continually experiment and improve his abilities in transforming wily minds and planting demons in causality.

Beyond this, Mo Hua continued to draw formations for the Canglang Sect, and in the process, became involved in more of the sect’s affairs.

He even joined the most central plans of the Canglang Sect, thanks to his outstanding performance.

Canglang City, outer city, on the city wall.

The Sect Leader of Canglang Sect pointed ahead at the vast clearing and the formation foundations being constructed, saying to Mo Hua:

"Young Master Mo, this is the future of our Canglang Sect..."

"This is a grand refinery shop. By leveraging the power of the entire Canglang City, it will be constructed without resting day or night, to be completed within three years and operational within five. It can forge a massive amount of spiritual artifacts, further advancing our Canglang Sect..."

The Sect Leader of Canglang Sect, while painting this vision for Mo Hua, added: "At the same time, this is also the future of Tongxian City."

Mo Hua was somewhat surprised, "Tongxian City?"

The Sect Leader of Canglang Sect nodded, "While building the refinery shop, I will expand the ’state road’ towards Big Black Mountain..."

The so-called state road refers to the "grand road" connecting the two state boundaries, wide enough for horses and carriages.

For a state boundary to develop and prosper, an interconnected "state road" is essential.

Both the grand refinery shop and the expansion of the state road are enormous projects.

Mo Hua couldn’t help but glance at the Sect Leader of Canglang Sect, nodding slightly, thinking to himself that this Sect Leader was indeed a person of great ambition.

The Sect Leader of Canglang Sect sincerely said:

"Young Master Mo, your cultivation and formation skills are exceptional, please lend me a helping hand."

Mo Hua was somewhat moved, but clearly hesitant.
