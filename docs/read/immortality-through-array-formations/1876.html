Chapter 1876: Chapter 923: Loyalty_2

Of course, he only entertained such thoughts in his mind.

The genuine act of Planting Devil in Taoist Heart is a method of the Demon Path, where wily minds split and sow a Demon Thought to manipulate others.

Those who have been manipulated become "puppets" of wily minds, with their Divine Thoughts infected, and given time, will inevitably die.

This kind of wicked Taoist Skill is something he would never use.

"However, this is also a train of thought... If I can’t use Planting Devil in Taoist Heart to ’manipulate’, then why not use Original Magnet information to ’induce’?"

Mo Hua, who trained in the Dao of Divine Thoughts, deeply understood that human cognition is easily "induced", and one’s Taoist Heart is always fluctuating.

Especially when there’s a cognitive barrier or an information gap.

"Utilizing Original Magnet for communication to induce cognition, ’tricking’ a Demon Sect disciple to act according to my plan..."

In a sense, this is also a form of "manipulation".

Mo Hua’s eyes brightened, and an idea slowly formed in his mind.

Afterwards, Mo Hua spent some time figuring out the plan.

However, to accomplish this plan, there are still many problems to overcome, especially regarding the Thunder Magnetism Formation.

His current level in Thunder Magnetism Formation is still lacking. There are many aspects of Thunder Magnetism he is still unclear about.

Mo Hua took some time to have tea with Elder Zheng, seeking advice on insights into formations related to Thunder Magnetism.

After returning to the Taixu Gate, he researched on his own and personally attempted it several times, ensuring his ideas were sound and his application of Thunder Magnetism was skilled, only then did he finally feel at ease.

Several days later, with everything ready, Mo Hua began to take action.

He first selected a candidate.

This "candidate" is crucial to the plan, responsible for helping him exchange the Reversed Spirit Formation and delivering the Reversed Spirit Formation out of the Demon Sect into his hands.

This candidate’s status couldn’t be too high, not above a Golden Core Elder.

Such a figure, he was currently incapable of "controlling".

Once a failure occurs, the rebound would be greater.

However, this candidate’s status couldn’t be too low either, preferably below an Elder, at the elite level of a Skill Transmission Disciple.

Mo Hua "monitored" the chats of Demon Sect disciples, and after several days of selection, he finally chose a suitable candidate:

Li San.

This name was rather ordinary.

This person had a Peak Foundation Establishment Cultivation, true name unknown, only known by the surname Li, ranking third in the Blood Cultivation Skill sequence, and was called "Li San".

Li San had deep standing in the Demon Sect and strong cultivation. Once he reached Golden Core, he had the qualification to be promoted to "Elder".

And his cultivation was only a step away from Golden Core.

Having mixed in the Demon Sect for a long time, he must have accumulated considerable Demon Merit, and exchanging the Reversed Spirit Formation worth three thousand points shouldn’t be a problem.

After the exchange, it’s also not easy to arouse suspicion.

Most importantly, he was greedy.

There was not a single Demon Sect cultivator who was not greedy, and Li San was particularly greedy.

Greed is the human nature that is best exploited.

Mo Hua, under the guise of "Elder You", sent a message to Li San:

"Do you want to become an Elder?"

Demon Sect disciples all yearn to become Elders, sometimes more intensely than those of the Righteous Dao.

The Blood Cultivation Skill they practice adheres to the "Skill Transmission" system.

The Elder transmitting skills sucks the blood of Skill Transmission disciples, and Skill Transmission disciples suck the blood of ordinary disciples, and ordinary disciples, in turn, suck the blood of innocent cultivators outside.

Disciples are all "exploited" by the Elders.

But once they become "Elders", it means gaining a certain degree of autonomy, no longer needing to "offer up" and possessing some "Skill Transmission" privileges.

The Demon Sect is unrestricted, and compared to ordinary sects, the power of an "Elder" is greater, allowing for more wanton actions.

Therefore, there is not a single disciple who doesn’t wish to become an Elder.

But becoming an Elder is not something they can do just because they want to.

The internal oppression within the Demon Sect is more direct, and the hierarchy is clearer. Each Elder has a spot, and to climb up, they must gain the recognition of other Elders.

Secondly, limited by the blood-sucking skill subjected to layer upon layer of exploitation, without permission from Golden Core Demon cultivators of "Elder" rank, and exclusive blood rewards from them, these Foundation Establishment Disciples attempting to break through to Golden Core is simply a pipe dream.

Therefore, the words "Elder" carry immense temptation.

In an unknown hideout of the Demon Sect.

Li San, who was thin, pale-faced, and had sharp, grim eyes, received Mo Hua’s message and immediately trembled all over, his whole body shaking with excitement.

The overwhelming joy surged through his heart.

The opportunity and possibility hinted at in the message made his long-anticipating heart become even more restless.

But at the same time, he couldn’t help but feel puzzled:

Why?

"Why did Elder You send this message to me? What does his venerable self mean by this? Does he want... to recommend me to be an Elder?"

"Could such a pie fall from the sky?"

Having muddled through the Demon Sect for many years, Li San, although greedy in nature, still retained necessary vigilance.

"Elder You, this disciple is foolish, and doesn’t know what you mean by this...?"

Li San pretended to be compliant.

But that message sank like a stone in the sea, with no response.

Li San felt uneasy, not knowing what Elder You’s intentions were.

Was he angry, or did something come up temporarily, leaving him no time to reply, or perhaps he was unsatisfied with my reply, too disdainful to respond?

Li San began to speculate Mo Hua’s intentions.

But the more he speculated, the more he felt at a loss.

He had no idea what schemes lay in the "Elder You" across from him.

He didn’t dare to ask personally.

He wasn’t willing to just ignore it either.

If by any chance this message was genuine... and Elder You truly intended to "promote" him, allowing him to become an Elder.

And if he acted coy, failing to show particular sincerity, it might cause Elder You to give up on him, thereby losing this "advancement" opportunity.
