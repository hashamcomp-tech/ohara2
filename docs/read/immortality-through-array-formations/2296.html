Chapter 2296: Chapter 1057: Great Wilderness Sacred Relic (Part 2)

"All methods are interconnected, the movement technique is exquisite, sword control across space, Formation Demon... it may seem varied, but at its core, it’s just two words..."

"Divine Sense!"

"Everything this child relies on is his Divine Sense!"

"No..."

Mr. Tu’s pupils suddenly contracted, his gaze stern.

Perhaps it’s just one word:

God!

...

The next day.

Great Wilderness Prison, in the dungeon.

The sky was already bright, but the prison remained dark, indistinguishable between day and night, nor white and black, people and demons.

Mr. Tu, clad in a pitch-black demon robe, held an ancient bronze box, walked into the eerie and frightening dungeon, and upon lifting his gaze, saw Mo Hua, covered with a small blanket, sleeping warmly and sweetly, his eyelids couldn’t help but twitch.

He stood there, holding the box, staring blankly like an "old servant" waiting for the "young master" to wake up and groom himself, but his gaze became increasingly icy.

After an unknown period, Mo Hua also woke up.

Being watched by Mr. Tu’s icy gaze, it was really hard to fall asleep again.

Mo Hua opened his mouth, sat up straight, stretched lazily, then got up, tidied up the small blanket, only then turned to look at Mr. Tu beside him, greeting:

"Good morning, Gentleman."

Mr. Tu said nothing.

Mo Hua feigned ignorance, asked: "By the way, I still don’t know your surname, Gentleman?"

"Tu." Mr. Tu replied coldly.

Mo Hua nodded, "Good morning, Mr. Tu."

Mr. Tu looked indifferently at Mo Hua, "I have brought you a gift."

"A gift?" Mo Hua was stunned.

Mr. Tu opened the bronze box, revealing the white bone circlet inside, and on the circlet, a milky white tooth, of unknown beast origin, and its age unknown, engraved with blood-red archaic script, covered in cracks.

"This is the supreme treasure personally refined by a master of my Great Wilderness Clan thousands of years ago, and now I’m giving it to you, try wearing it." Mr. Tu said coldly.

Mo Hua looked at the ancient bone circlet, his expression serious, "This gift... is too precious. Without merit, I cannot accept it."

Mr. Tu said: "A gift from an elder, one cannot decline."

Mo Hua humbly said: "I am unworthy."

Mr. Tu shook his head, "Among the countless cultivators in this entire Qian Learning State Boundary, and even among all the believers of the Divine Lord in this whole temple, only you are worthy of this supreme treasure."

Mr. Tu’s tone was appreciative, his attitude adamant.

Mo Hua, "unable to refuse such kindness," but not wanting this "bone frame" like evil object either, quietly asked Mr. Tu:

"This...what exactly is it? What’s it used for?"

Mr. Tu said gently: "Put it on your head, and you will know."

Mo Hua didn’t want to wear it, but seeing Mr. Tu’s expression, obviously if he didn’t wear it, he definitely wouldn’t let it go.

So Mo Hua attempted to clasp this tooth talisman circlet on his head.

The moment he clasped it on, the bone circlet quivered, like a monster beast opening its mouth, growing fangs, "biting" into Mo Hua’s head, embedding into acupoints like Tianchong, Fubai, Fengchi, Tianzhu, Yuzhen, Baihui and others.

And the ancient tooth talisman was like the fangs of an ancient fierce beast, biting just right on the Shenting Acupoint at Mo Hua’s forehead.

A force of all energies surged out, completely suppressing Mo Hua’s Sea of Consciousness.

He could no longer release his Divine Sense, and even his divinity was suppressed.

Mo Hua could no longer remain calm, immediately turning pale with shock, looked at Mr. Tu in terror, and shouted:

"What is this? Why am I, I..."

Mr. Tu’s expression was unreadable, "Why... can’t you communicate with the ’deity’ in your Sea of Consciousness anymore?"

Mo Hua’s face turned violently pale, lips tightly pressed together, not daring to say a word.

Seeing this, Mr. Tu nodded slightly, roughly confirming his previous suspicions.

There is a "Fierce God" in the Taixu Gate, the great enemy of the Divine Lord.

The Fierce God is also a deity, and since it is a deity, it must have something to "reside in," or someone to "parasite on."

Normal deities, dwell in things of the world, build a Divine Temple, nurture believers, steadfast as a mountain, widely dispensing grace, rarely moving at will.

But the Fierce God of the Taixu Gate is different, it is simply too "active."

It’s everywhere.

Mr. Tu speculated that the Fierce God should be residing in a flesh and blood body, even to say that it is "co-being" with a divine person is not too much.

The prime suspect of the Taixu Gate is naturally this Formation Leader with Super Divine Sense—Mo Hua.

But matters concerning deities are of grave concern and need extreme caution.

Mr. Tu pondered for a long time, after much deliberation yesterday, finally made the firm decision to resort to this sacred artifact, passed down from ancient times in Great Wilderness, to suppress the Evil God.

To use this "sacred artifact" to seal Mo Hua’s Divine Sense, suppress the "deity" on Mo Hua.

Deities are born of the Dao of heaven and earth, regardless of their grade, their very existence is on a different plane from human cultivators.

Moreover, the deity of the Taixu Gate is a "Fierce God" of countless killings.

The brief encounter in the Valley of Ten Thousand Demons left Mr. Tu with a deep shadow.

Without the Divine Lord’s mighty power, Mr. Tu couldn’t deal with this Fierce God.

Fortunately, this Fierce God has a fatal weakness—that is Mo Hua, whom it parasitizes.

Controlling the person it parasitizes naturally enables control over the deity.

The parasitized person is both the "puppet" through which the deity walks the earth, and the deity’s most fatal "seal."

Now, with Mo Hua "sealed," the Fierce God can no longer stir up trouble.

Conversely, with the Fierce God isolated, it rendered Mo Hua "useless."

Mr. Tu looked again at the panicked Mo Hua, a slight cold smile on his lips.

This is a filthy brat with exceedingly good luck.

Not sure what fortuitous event he encountered to receive the deity’s favor in his youth, thereby "co-being" with the deity.
