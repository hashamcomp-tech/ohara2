And many Sects were originally "subordinate" to the Qian Taoist Sect, following its lead.

Other Sects were "brother" Sects of the Sever Gold Sect and Gui Water Sect, with intertwined interests.

These related forces also had poor relations with the Taixu Gate.

Now, Tai’a, Chongxu, and Taixu have united into one Sect.

All these accumulated grudges and enmities naturally converged in one place.

The Taixu Gate naturally became the "target of all."

These Sect grudges are but one aspect.

Another aspect is due to the current position of the Taixu Gate.

Being the head of the Eight Great Gates is actually a hot potato.

Advance, and it threatens the Four Great Sects, becoming a "thorn in the eye" for the Four Great Sects.

Retreat, and all the Sects of the Eight Great Gates vie for the top position, and the Taixu Gate’s current position becomes a "fat piece of meat" in everyone’s eyes.

If the Eight Great Gates want to advance, their first target is the Taixu Gate, ranked first among the Eight Gates.

The Four Great Sects, not wanting to fall back, also see the Taixu Gate, one step away from them, as their primary threat.

For these reasons, the Taixu Gate naturally finds itself in a precarious situation.

Not to mention, within the Taixu Gate, there’s Mo Hua.

The disciples participating in the sword discussion hold a grudge against Mo Hua because of victory and defeat.

The elders outside the arena, especially the Elder of Daoist Law, also hold a grudge against Mo Hua in their hearts.

Currently, on the grand stage of the Sects, Taixu Gate is the target of everyone’s attacks.

Among the Sect’s disciples, Mo Hua is also the public "archenemy"...

So much so, that many disciples are eagerly anticipating this Shura battle.
