Chapter 2201: Chapter 1028: Tumo Treasure Book (2)

Seeing that with just a few more wins, Taixu Gate’s ranking is about to slip again, falling from the top position among the Eight Great Gates.

Taixu Gate is naturally unwilling.

Not only Mo Hua, but several other teams of Taixu Gate disciples are also fighting fiercely, nearly exhausting themselves in every battle, very brutal.

During this time, the Tai’a Five Brothers, Yang Qianjun, and several other teams also desperately won a few matches.

These victories did not come easy. But compared to the overall situation, they seem somewhat like a drop in the ocean.

The overall gap between Taixu Gate and Daluo Sect has not widened.

In this case, Mo Hua can’t afford to lose.

Afterward, the Dao Discussion Heaven Instrument continued to simulate the roster.

Mo Hua competed in two Earth Rank sword discussions, one against a top disciple of Lingxiao Sect, and the other against a top-tier but not elite team of Qian Taoist Sect.

These two teams, though not the top in strength, are also definitely not weak, and must not be underestimated.

Mo Hua’s tactical approach is still centered around "Formation," assisting Linghu Xiao and the others in attack.

He knows too many formations.

Within the Second Grade, master of all Five Elements and Eight Trigrams.

Killing Formations, Defensive Formations, Trapping Formations, mastered all.

Before the battle, covering it with a small blanket, no one knows which formations he will secretly draw.

Any formation is possible.

With the terrain and competition format, adapting to the circumstances, ever-changing, impossible to defend against.

And Linghu Xiao and the others, after so many sword discussion trials, have made substantial progress in both strength and mentality.

With the additional support of Mo Hua’s formations.

This team of Taixu Gate is indeed already half a step to "top-tier" strong teams’ level.

So, despite some twists and turns in the sword discussions with Lingxiao Sect and Qian Taoist Sect, they still secured victory.

But even though they won the sword discussions, Mo Hua had a bad premonition.

Through these two sword discussions, he vaguely felt that his conventional formation routines seemed to have been figured out...

Even though in previous sword discussions, he restrained himself enough and didn’t excessively use formations.

Most formations were used at critical moments.

When drawing formations, he still covered them with a small blanket.

But these seem to no longer be effective.

Mo Hua can vaguely sense that when other teams responded to his formations, their every move formed a set of rules similar to "guidelines."

These guidelines may go unnoticed by others, but not by the meticulous and divinely sensitive Mo Hua.

And these "guidelines" could not possibly have been devised by these disciples.

Because their formation skills are far from reaching that level.

Mo Hua pondered for a long time, vaguely suspecting that this was likely the work of a certain "Elder of Formation Arts"...

Or perhaps, a group of "Elders of Formation Arts"...

Gathering together, dividing and cooperating, combining the sword discussion terrain and competition format to simulate his formation routines and styles.

This simulation is similar to a real combat rehearsal.

Using Divine Sense simulation, assessing which formations would be used in the sword discussion based on the terrain and the opponent...

Mo Hua took a slight breath of cold air.

"It can’t be..."

"Would they really go to such lengths for just a small disciple like me?"

But his experience and intuition shouldn’t be wrong.

There definitely is a "guideline" in the actions of his sword discussion opponents.

As for where this "guideline" came from, who created it, what form it takes, and what content it contains...

Mo Hua couldn’t guess at the moment, and it wasn’t easy to deduce.

But soon, he didn’t have to guess.

Two days later, someone handed this "guideline" directly to him.

In the dining hall, Mo Hua looked at the white jade slip in his hand, lost in deep thought.

The name of the jade slip is:

"Rules for Mo Hua."

Its full name: "Behavior Guidelines for the Various Insidious Tactics Targeting Mo Hua During the Sword Discussion Conference."

This guideline was given to him by Song Jian.

According to Song Jian, "All sects hostile to Taixu Gate, or those that lost to you in the Sword Discussion Conference, have this guideline."

"Ordinary disciples do not have it."

"But among the Four Great Sects and the Eight Great Gates, any Heavenly Pride Disciple at the Earth Rank who can participate in the Sword Discussion Conference, almost all have a copy now."

Sever Gold Sect, because of Mo Hua, has a deep feud with Taixu Gate, belonging to a "hostile" sect.

This year’s Sword Discussion Conference, again because of Mo Hua, Sever Gold Sect lost many matches to Taixu Gate and suffered a terrible defeat.

Also, due to Mo Hua’s "behind-the-scenes manipulation," Song Jian is now a "top-ranked" Heavenly Pride Disciple of Sever Gold Sect.

Therefore, this "Rules for Mo Hua" was passed to Song Jian.

And ultimately forwarded to Mo Hua himself.

The cause and effect cycle, very clever.

Of course, looking at this "guideline for himself," Mo Hua’s feelings were quite mixed.

In the dining hall, it wasn’t just Mo Hua looking.

Cheng Mo, Situ Jian, a group of Junior Brothers including Hao Xuan, also gathered their heads, looking at the "Rules for Mo Hua" in Mo Hua’s hand.

Even Linghu Xiao, who came to Taixu Mountain to freeload a meal by chance, couldn’t help but sneak a glance over.

The core purpose of the "Rules for Mo Hua" is to target Mo Hua.

Its contents are divided into many Chapters: including the "Spell Chapter," "Movement Technique Chapter," "Formation Chapter," and a page of "Mind Strategy Chapter."

The Mind Strategy Chapter has the fewest words, mainly involving some anonymous sect disciples recounting their experiences of being "deceived" as a warning.

Including: "Do not talk to Mo Hua before the battle;"

"Do not believe Mo Hua’s slander;"

"Mo Hua is extremely insidious, every word is meant to achieve his tactical objectives, and none of it would be true;"
