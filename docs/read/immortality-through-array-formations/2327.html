Chapter 2327: Chapter 1067: Ambition (Part 2)

"Yes."

"Since you can draw ground into formation, you should easily understand..." Mr. Tu said slowly:

"Ordinary formation media is generic, and it’s not difficult to draw formations on it;"

"But some secret formations involve specialized formation media. Trying to inscribe formation patterns on these specialized media is not that simple."

Mo Hua slightly nodded upon hearing this.

Drawing ground into formation, using ’earth’ as the medium, requires communicating with Earth Dao Meaning, it’s not so easily mastered.

Mr. Tu looked up at the massive skeleton before him and continued:

"This Desolate Heaven Blood Sacrifice Formation is the same. Without the specific secret technique of the Great Wilderness Witch, your formation patterns cannot remain on this ancient beast skeleton."

"In the inheritance system of the Great Wilderness, this ancient beast skeleton is not simply a ’formation media’, it is also a part of the formation, blending as one with the formation eye, formation pivot, and even the overall formation patterns."

"If you merely regard it as a simple ’media’, consider it a ’dead thing’, then the formation patterns you draw will be useless, unable to unleash the power of the Blood Sacrifice Formation."

Mo Hua frowned and asked, "Then how should I draw it?"

Mr. Tu remained silent for a moment before slowly saying, "This is what I’m going to teach you next, the Great Wilderness Bone Carving Technique."

"Great Wilderness Bone Carving?" Mo Hua was taken aback.

"It’s a technique of the Great Wilderness Witch that inscribes formations onto the bones, allowing the formations to merge as one with the skeleton."

Mr. Tu looked at Mo Hua, coldly saying, "I won’t unnecessarily explain anything, nor do you need to ask more questions. Just learn the method and inscribe the formation well."

Having something to learn was good, Mo Hua stopped asking more questions and simply nodded.

Mr. Tu stepped up, walking along the white bone spine to climb the enormous ancient beast skeleton, speaking as he walked:

"This Great Wilderness Bone Carving Technique doesn’t use a formation pen but a knife pen. Utilizing the sharpness of the blade, you inscribe formation patterns onto the white bones, integrating formation patterns and bones, melding ink with media, inseparable pattern and bone."

"When inscribing, you must use the blood of the ancient beast, engrave the ancient beast’s bone, feel the soul of the ancient beast, and use your own divine thought as media; only by blending all three as one can a formation be completed."

"Moreover, there are many taboos..."

...

Mr. Tu continued walking and speaking, teaching Mo Hua the "Great Wilderness Bone Carving Technique" in its entirety, and then handed Mo Hua an ancient knife pen and a bottle of scarlet blood ink, pointing at a blank bone ground, saying:

"Try it."

Mo Hua understood Mr. Tu’s intention, and according to his method, sensed the beast soul, controlled the knife pen with divine thought, merged monster blood, and laid down a stroke.

A scarlet formation pattern was inscribed onto the bone.

An eerie wind blew, the blood merged with the bone, becoming one.

Mr. Tu turned to look at Mo Hua, his expression solemn, his pupils shrinking like needles, revealing disbelief.

Mo Hua looked up at Mr. Tu and asked, "Is it like this?"

Mr. Tu slowly nodded, "Yes."

Suppressing various emotions, he added: "Draw according to what I said, the faster you draw, the better."

Mo Hua nodded, "Alright."

Then he began, from the place planned by Mr. Tu, using the knife pen, dotting monster blood, sensing beast soul, inscribing stroke by stroke the blood sacrifice formation pivot.

Initially, he drew slowly, inscribing carefully, each stroke was well-considered, slow but steady, with no errors.

The second stroke was still slow but slightly faster than the first.

The third stroke was faster than the second.

Then came the fourth stroke...

Each stroke grew faster, becoming more and more proficient.

After only a few dozen strokes, he transformed from a "novice" to a "veteran," his attitude calm and composed, his strokes easy and fluid.

Mr. Tu took a deep breath, feeling tremors in his heart.

Few people understand the Great Wilderness Bone Carving Technique.

The blood sacrifice formation pivot is even controlled solely by him.

Yet now, this young man had clearly just learned, just grasped, and just drawn, visually growing to this point...

"Whatever he’s taught, he can master."

"Whatever he learns, he can immediately apply..."

"Such talent, what exactly is this... creature?"

Mr. Tu finally gave Mo Hua a deep look, jealousy and apprehension flashing briefly before fading into calmness.

Beneath the calm, there lay an intent to kill.

Mo Hua seemed unaware of this, engrossed in using the "Great Wilderness Bone Carving Technique," inscribing the formation with focused dedication, losing perception of everything around him.

Mr. Tu’s form dissolved into a blood mist, gradually fading away, then disappeared.

Mo Hua squatted on the ancient beast skeleton, following Mr. Tu’s instructions, focused on inscribing the Blood Sacrifice Formation, drawing faster and more skillfully...

With Mo Hua’s help, Mr. Tu could finally spare a hand to handle the Taoist Court’s Mystic Sky Beidou Seven Star Formation.

Likewise, with Mo Hua’s assistance, the core progression of the Blood Sacrifice Array finally began to advance again...

Situ True Man shook his head, sighing: "It’s different..."

"The Blood Sacrifice Array is a ritual formation, involving the Divine Thought Path, different from typical formations."

"The outer evil formation might be inscribed by evil formation masters to fill the formation’s volume."

"But the core formation eye, and formation pivot, can only be controlled by one person."

"Just like a sacrificial ritual can only have one ’chief priest.’ This kind of Blood Sacrifice Array can only have one person to hold the deepest secrets."
