Vol 2 Chapter 52 Linlang's Course

"Now, you should understand why the Third Tier is called the most important stage."

"Yes, thank you for explaining." Sylutia expressed her gratitude, a sense of curiosity also rising within her.

"Later, you will learn more about the core secret arts of the Third Tier. This scholar here will also provide you with individual and meticulous guidance."

"Ah, thank you." Sylutia hadn't expected the association to specially assign a 'Fifth Tier' big shot to teach her how to advance.

"Considering you have just recently stepped into the Second Tier, there's no need to be in such a hurry just yet. You can also use this stage to try out the secret arts and philosophies of different schools, choosing the path that best suits you for advancement." This Knight Captain truly had noble character, showing no particular bias or intention to guide her in a specific direction.

"I understand."

"Once you have determined your direction, you can inform us of your choice. After that, we will also prepare the necessary resources for you. However, the final trial will still need to be completed by yourself."

"I understand."

"Good, the important matters have been covered. Next is about your rewards and your mission objectives for the second half of the year."

"Considering your advancement focused on the cultivation of 'Alfalfa,' the task for the second half of the year will be to entrust you with providing a sufficient amount of alfalfa fodder. This shouldn't be difficult for you, right?" This was practically giving away points. For Sylutia, cultivating alfalfa was currently nearly ten times easier than cultivating wheat. Just by living in the valley, she could continuously produce 'Perfect' quality alfalfa.

"Additionally, we have also reserved a few extra commendation tasks for you."

"Submit 3000 Jin of Perfect quality wheat, and our Knight Order will award you with a set of 'White Knight Armor' suitable for your physique."

"If you are willing to help us pasture foals in the valley, for every First-Tier Aspect foal you raise, we will reward you with one Glittergold ingot."

"Learn and master a set of the Knight Order's advanced swordsmanship. If completed, you will be awarded a 'Second Tier · Rare' knight's sword."

Although they wouldn't openly poach her, this Knight Captain's tasks and rewards clearly guided this young girl towards the knight's path. Regarding this situation, the expressions of the other few people in the hall varied. That scholar seemed to be used to it, as it had nothing to do with him, a faint smile hanging on his face. The burly blacksmith man nearby also pretended not to hear. In his view, with Sylutia's slender and delicate arms, she definitely wouldn't make it as a blacksmith, so he held no expectations either.

Only Kuland Hill on the other side had a slightly twitching expression. He had some things he wanted to say, but considering the status of the person at the head of the table and the fact that the other's actions were still within the scope of his duties, it was also inappropriate to make a scene in front of a junior.

The conversation ended here. Afterwards, Sylutia left the tower and returned to the association's room to stay temporarily.

The next day, she met yesterday's scholar, Seraph, in a tea room within the association. What surprised Sylutia somewhat was that she saw another familiar girl of similar age beside this scholar.

"Hello, my name is Softina." She also recognized this girl;she was the one who shared the carriage ride when returning to the Glittergold Plains that day.

"Hello." Sylutia returned the greeting with a nod, examining the other party with slight curiosity.

Softina was about the same age as her, probably around 17 years old as well. She wore a black velvet dress with a white silk scarf at the collar, exuding a gentle and intellectual aura.

"This is my daughter. She also happens to be at the Second Tier right now, so she should have quite a lot in common with Tia." Seraph introduced.

Afterwards, Seraph inquired about Sylutia's learning progress and briefly tested her grasp of various secret histories.

After understanding that Sylutia's foundation was solid with no omissions, he praised her quite admiringly, then told the young girl what she would need to learn next.

"I will further teach you the history of the deeper world. That is, the various organizations that influenced the world's situation behind the rise and fall of each kingdom—their origins, development, and the rise and decline of various secret arts they brought about. Much of this knowledge is not allowed to be circulated or printed in the surface world. However, since you have come to the Hidden Grove and stepped into the Second Tier, you now have the qualification to know."

"I am stationed in the city long-term. You will need to set aside three days each week to come to my place for lessons until you finish the curriculum I teach."

"Understood, thank you, instructor."

"Additionally, I also offer some small courses. However, according to the rules of the Hidden Grove, these must also be charged. Later, you can look at the course list." After saying this, he had Softina beside him take out a neatly printed page from a briefcase and hand it to the young girl.

[Ancient Furlian Language Study, 48 classes—420 gold coins]

[Fairy Language Study, 36 classes—600 gold coins]

[Elf Language Study, 54 classes—520 gold coins]

[Beastfolk Language Study (one category), 32 classes—300 gold coins]

[Basic Rune Knowledge, 24 classes—240 gold coins]

[Intermediate Rune Knowledge (mid-Eighth Epoch flourishing school), 36 classes—480 gold coins]

[Advanced Rune Knowledge (late-Eighth Epoch classic school), 48 classes—1200 gold coins]

[Glass Rune Knowledge (Six-Part Glazed Glass School), 64 classes—3600 gold coins]

[Seven Gates and Keys (Swan Bright Light School), 42 classes—4800 gold coins]

[Introduction to Military Command, 12 classes—200 gold coins]

[Noble Etiquette and Heritage, 18 classes—240 gold coins]

[World Within the Pupil (study of seven basic pupil arts), 16 classes—360 gold coins]

[Luo Bai's Garden (notes on the improvement process of 12 special crops), 28 classes—600 gold coins]

[The Ancient King Mureto (Primordial King Epic), 6 classes—100 gold coins]

[The Rise of the Blood Dynasty (includes 6 Bloodline secret arts, three rituals), 27 classes—720 gold coins]

...

Gazing at the dazzling array of course lists, Sylutia's eyes stared blankly, feeling a thirst for knowledge, but seeing the rows of prices at the back, her heart also turned cold.

So expensive, she quietly complained to herself.

But, is this the price of knowledge?

Hearing Sylutia's quiet complaint, the girl opposite her couldn't help but cover her mouth with a light laugh.

"Although the price is not cheap, it is absolutely worth more than its value." Softina's voice was clear and crisp.

"Actually, this is already the price list after the association's preferential subsidies. Generally, for these courses, if the number of registrants is insufficient, the instructor won't open the class."

For a Fifth Tier Transcendent, there are many things they can do. Teaching low-level apprentices is something that only happens if the association specifically assigns the task or provides sufficient subsidies. Otherwise, if they spent this time doing other things, the returns would be higher.

In fact, if it weren't for the association's designated assignment, a figure like Seraph wouldn't be here. He usually stays stationed at several famous academic sites within the Hidden Grove, exchanging and learning with colleagues while also guiding those Third or Fourth Tier Transcendents who come seeking knowledge.
