Chapter 47

A gentle breeze blew through, dispersing the overflowing Blazing Sun heat from the flower field and bringing a touch of refreshing coolness. However, the scattered Blazing Sun power did not vanish; instead, it condensed onto the countless petals filling the sky, emitting a radiant halo under the sunlight.

These petals danced and swirled, arranging themselves into a vortex-like ring that continuously gathered toward the sky. They pressed tightly against each other, compressed together, and finally solidified, weaving into an exquisite bracelet made of pale golden petals.

This delicate bracelet slowly descended from the air, eventually hovering quietly before Sylutia, emanating a faint warmth and glow.

[Blazing Sun Petal Bracelet] (Second Tier · Blazing Sun): Formed by condensing, compressing, and arranging petals imbued with the 'Blazing Sun' Aspect, creating natural engraved patterns that allow the wearer to dispel chill, restore stamina, and remain energetic. (Resists erosion effects from 'Slumbering Winter,' 'Nightmare,' and 'Mist Shadow'; can accelerate stamina recovery and maintain warmth)

"Consider this my first meeting gift to you, how about that."

Her pale blue eyes seemed to swirl with a faint golden halo, stabilizing and condensing the Aspect power within the bracelet before her.

"Thank you, Lady Franne." Gently placing the potted plant she was holding beside her, the young girl bowed in gratitude.

Soon after, she slightly freed one hand, and the golden petal bracelet floated lightly toward her, slipping onto her fair wrist.

As the bracelet settled, a gentle warmth instantly filled her entire body. The previous scorching sensation from being in the sea of flowers dissipated completely, no longer assaulting her with waves of heat.

Pleased with Sylutia's acceptance, the noblewoman showed satisfaction on her face, nodding slightly. Then, holding her parasol, she walked past the girl.

"Follow me."

Thus, Sylutia walked behind her as if in a dream. The flower field brimming with golden light before her eyes resembled a heavenly world. It wasn't until they gradually left this sunflower field and entered the cool, comfortable mansion that she slowly snapped out of her daze.

Arriving at another quiet room, Lady Franne had Sylutia place the glass-covered potted plant on the table.

"Just put it there. You must be tired from carrying it." By now, she had already placed the parasol by the door, and her elegantly tied flowing golden hair came into the girl's view.

"Yes."

Shortly after setting down the potted plant, the noblewoman lightly raised her finger, and the black cloth covering it floated up gracefully before slowly settling on a nearby shelf.

Inside the glass cover, the quietly blooming tender green Swan Orchid appeared before both of them.

Raising her hand again, the glass cover slowly lifted and was placed beside the table. At this moment, the unique fragrance of the 'Swan Orchid' gradually diffused, refreshing the atmosphere in the room.

Gazing at this blooming 'Swan Orchid,' a faint golden halo swirled in Lady Franne's eyes. She seemed to possess another observation method that allowed her to detect the plant's condition.

"Excellent, one might even say perfect, especially for someone who hasn't yet stepped into the First Tier." She gently unfolded a folding fan with one hand, allowing the aura brewing within the Swan Orchid's stamens to spread throughout the room, dispelling the Blazing Sun's heat.

"Such an environment is indeed what I need." The noblewoman blinked, smiling at the young girl.

"Sit down. It seems we'll be having a good conversation today."

The two sat facing each other, and the noblewoman lightly clapped her hands again, summoning the young maid.

"Lining, prepare 'Snow Orchid Flower Tea' for Tia and me," she instructed.

"Yes, madam." The maid slightly crouched in a curtsy before leaving to prepare.

While they waited, Lady Franne observed the young girl with a look of considerable interest.

"Regarding your situation, I've heard about it from Mareen and also received letters from Scholar Heide."

"Although I rarely socialize around Scorchstone City, I always manage to easily learn about many things that happen here."

"Breathing Technique third stage, Dew Prayer Method third stage. Your progress speed exceeds many people's expectations." As she said this, the maid entered the room carrying tea, placing two cups of white tea with steeping flowers and leaves in front of them.

"Have some. Snow Orchid Flower Tea has a cool taste, perfect for dispelling the afternoon heat." Lady Franne picked up her teacup.

Sylutia obediently picked up hers as well, taking small sips.

The taste was slightly cool and sweet, followed by a pleasant mellow thickness. It seemed to contain filtered milk and special petals, making the tea's flavor exceptional.

Afterward, Sylutia set down her teacup and explained her purpose to Lady Franne.

"Regarding the advancement path and knowledge of 'gardener,' I can teach you everything I know." Lady Franne agreed to this very readily.

"However, gardener is a unique profession where knowledge doesn't play a huge role. In fact, Scholar Heide's understanding of gardening-related knowledge isn't much less than mine, and he has an extensive collection of books."

"Yet, that doesn't mean he can become an exceptional gardener."

"The flowers and plants in this world are incredibly diverse, each with different temperaments, environments, experiences, and seasons. Relying solely on rigid book knowledge, you can only become a competent florist at best."

"Therefore, having keen insight, possessing meticulousness and patience, along with time for cultivation, are what determine whether a gardener becomes outstanding."

"It brings me great joy to see such a talented junior like you." She took another small sip of tea before setting the cup down.

"If you're willing, you can learn gardener-related knowledge here with me. But as tuition, you'll also need to help me manage the flower fields and handle some simple tasks."

"Occasionally, when I travel far, I'll need you to stay here regularly. Although Lining is a very capable maid, her expertise in gardening is rather average."

"I wonder if you'd be willing?"

Sylutia thought for a moment, found nothing inappropriate about it, and even sensed the kindness and appreciation the other conveyed, so she nodded in agreement.

"Excellent. It seems this quiet mansion of mine is about to become livelier." She smiled slightly.

"Come with me. I'll first show you around to familiarize you with the environment, then explain your usual duties."

Thus, she led Sylutia out of the room again, navigating through the complex and spacious mansion.

"First, regarding watering the flower fields: Lining usually handles the watering, but she isn't skilled at gardening. So, you'll need to come once a week to check the flower fields' condition, make fine adjustments for each sunflower's situation, supplement watering, or temporarily hold back."

"Second, some sunflowers in the field need regular pruning to remove excess and poorly developing branches and leaves. Since you can cultivate Swan Orchids so well, you should easily identify areas of stunted growth and excess."

After listening, Sylutia continued to nod slightly, indicating her understanding.

"Finally, you'll represent me in receiving some visitors."

"Since I'm not home every day and I'm too lazy to interact with some guests, you can handle trivial requests as you see fit. If there's anything you don't understand, just ask Lining directly; she knows my usual practices."

"This..." Sylutia showed hesitation on her face. Could she really handle the complex affairs of those visitors?

"Don't worry. Besides, you'll eventually encounter similar matters." Lady Franne gently waved her folding fan.

"Oh, later, have Lining take your measurements. I'll find a suitable tailor to make you several sets of clothes."

"Although your plain white dress suits your temperament well, here, I prefer vibrant tender yellows and brilliant golds."

And so, Sylutia's life for the coming period was arranged. She had a premonition that here with Lady Franne, she would encounter many previously unknown knowledge and social circles.
