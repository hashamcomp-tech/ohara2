Chapter 464: Chapter 464 Tatiana Show III

The Lamborghini moved smoothly through the night traffic, its engine humming with controlled power as the city lights streaked across the glossy windshield. Tatiana was still watching the road ahead with a quiet smile when Liam suddenly spoke again.

"Yeah," he said casually, "cross over and take the wheel."

For a moment she thought she misheard him.

Then she realized he was completely serious.

Tatiana nodded quickly and reached for her seatbelt, pressing the release button. The belt slid back with a soft snap as Liam also eased his foot off the accelerator. The powerful car gradually slowed down until they were moving at a comfortable and safe speed among the other vehicles on the road.

Liam unbuckled his own seatbelt as well.

"Grab the wheel first," he told her calmly.

Tatiana leaned forward slightly and stretched her hands toward the steering wheel. Her fingers wrapped around the leather surface and she felt the firmness of the grip immediately. Even just touching the wheel made her heart beat a little faster.

"Okay," Liam said. "Now come over."

Tatiana slid toward the center while Liam leaned down and shifted beneath her. His body moved carefully between the seats while keeping the car steady. It was clumsy for a second but within moments they had switched places.

Tatiana settled into the driver’s seat.

The moment she sat there she felt a strange rush of adrenaline.

The steering wheel was now fully in her hands.

The dashboard lights glowed in front of her and the road stretched ahead under the city lights.

For a second she simply stared forward.

"I’ve never driven something like this before," she admitted.

Her hands tightened slightly around the wheel as she tried to get used to the feel of it.

It was very different from the normal cars she had driven before. Everything felt more sensitive. The steering was lighter yet more precise and even the slightest movement of her foot on the pedals made the car react instantly.

Liam noticed the tension in her shoulders.

"Relax," he said calmly from the passenger seat.

His voice was steady and reassuring.

"I’ll help you."

Tatiana let out a slow breath and nodded.

"Okay."

Liam leaned slightly toward the dashboard and pointed at the controls.

"First thing you should know is that this car responds very quickly. Don’t press the accelerator too hard. Just ease into it."

Tatiana nodded while keeping her eyes on the road.

"Like this?" she asked as she gently pressed the pedal.

The Lamborghini moved forward smoothly with a low growl from the engine.

"Exactly," Liam said.

He continued explaining the basics quickly but clearly. He showed her how the gear paddle behind the steering wheel worked and how the driving mode could adjust the car’s responsiveness. He pointed out the digital speed display and the navigation interface glowing on the center console.

Tatiana listened carefully.

At first her movements were cautious but within a couple of minutes she began to feel the rhythm of the car.

Her steering became smoother.

Her foot controlled the accelerator with more confidence.

The Lamborghini flowed through the traffic lanes with elegant precision.

Liam watched her for a moment before nodding with approval.

"You’re getting it."

Tatiana allowed herself a small smile.

"Yeah," she admitted quietly. "It’s actually easier than I thought."

The powerful machine responded to her like it was built for her hands.

Liam leaned back into his seat.

"Follow the map on the dashboard."

Tatiana glanced down at the navigation screen where a glowing route pointed toward their destination.

But then she shook her head.

"I don’t need the map."

Her eyes returned to the road ahead.

"I know where we are going."

Liam looked at her briefly before nodding.

"Alright."

He slipped his hand casually into his pocket for a moment.

Inside his fingers brushed against a small object. He checked it quickly just to make sure it was still there.

The small bottle.

Satisfied, he left it in place and withdrew his hand.

Tatiana never noticed the gesture.

Her entire focus was on driving the car.

The wind rushed gently along the sides of the Lamborghini while the city lights reflected across its bright orange body as it moved through the streets.

Five minutes later the scenery began to change.

The busy streets gradually gave way to a more private area lined with darker buildings and guarded entrances.

Soon a large gate appeared ahead.

Tatiana slowed the car as they approached.

The sign above the entrance confirmed the place.

Shadow Lounge.

Two armed guards standing at the gate immediately turned their heads when they saw the bright orange Lamborghini approaching.

Their reaction was instant.

Both men stepped forward as the car rolled to a stop in front of them.

Tatiana lowered the window slowly.

The guards leaned down slightly to look inside the vehicle.

The moment their eyes landed on Liam sitting in the passenger seat their expressions changed immediately.

Their faces lit up with recognition.

"Mr," one of them said quickly with a respectful nod. "Welcome back."

Tatiana’s eyes shifted sideways toward Liam.

He simply smiled calmly.

"Do you still need to verify my identity?" he asked casually.

The guards shook their heads almost instantly.

Both of them looked oddly pleased.

"There’s no such thing for you young master," the other guard said with a grin.

"You’re free to enter."

Tatiana’s fingers tightened slightly on the steering wheel.

Young master?

Her brain struggled to process what she was hearing.

The guards stepped back and pressed a control panel beside the gate.

The heavy metal doors slowly slid open.

All throughout the short interaction Tatiana kept her eyes facing forward.

Her face remained calm but inside her mind was exploding with confusion.

She slowly pressed the accelerator and drove the Lamborghini through the gate.

The car rolled into the private compound smoothly.

Only after they had passed the entrance did she finally turn her head toward Liam.

Her voice dropped low.

"Okay."

She stared at him with narrowed eyes.

"What the fuck was that?"

Tatiana slowly guided the Lamborghini deeper into the compound until she reached the parking area beside the building. Even here the place looked expensive. Luxury cars were already lined up neatly in rows under the soft glow of overhead lights. Sleek sedans, a few sports cars, and several high end SUVs sat quietly like silent trophies belonging to whoever had come for the night’s party.

She eased the car into an empty spot and gently pressed the brake.

The engine went quiet.

For a moment neither of them moved.

Tatiana turned off the ignition and the powerful machine finally settled into silence. The excitement of the drive was still buzzing in her veins but that was not the thing occupying her mind anymore.

Her attention shifted slowly to Liam.

He opened his door and stepped out casually as if nothing unusual had just happened.

Tatiana stepped out on her side too, closing the door behind her. The cool evening air wrapped around them again and the distant music from the club area could already be heard faintly through the walls of the building.

She walked around the front of the car and stopped a few feet away from Liam.

Her expression said everything.

Her eyes were fixed on him with a clear demand.

Explain.

She did not even bother hiding it.

Liam saw the look immediately.

For a moment he simply smiled.

The calm mysterious smile that had been driving her crazy the entire day.

"I will tell you when we get to the room," he said lightly.

Tatiana blinked.

"Room?"

Her eyebrow lifted slowly.

Liam glanced at his wristwatch while nodding.

"Yes."

He checked the time carefully before continuing.

"We still have about an hour before the party starts."

Tatiana folded her arms slightly while listening.

"I placed a room down so I can explain my plan to you."

She stared at him for a moment.

Her brain was still trying to process everything that had happened in the last few hours. First the green access card. Then the Lamborghini. Then the guards at the gate calling him young master like he owned the place.

Now apparently he had already rented a room inside this building as well.

"Okay," she said awkwardly.

The word came out slowly as if she still had several questions she did not know how to ask yet.

Liam walked back toward the car again.

Tatiana frowned slightly.

"What are you doing?"

He opened the rear compartment and reached inside.

A moment later he pulled out a large black duffle bag.

Tatiana’s eyes widened slightly.

She had not even noticed that bag earlier.

The car had been so stunning that she never paid attention to anything else inside it.

Now she watched Liam lift the bag easily with one hand before closing the compartment again.

He locked the Lamborghini with the key.

The car responded with a short beep and the mirrors folded inward slightly.

Then he looked at her.

"Let’s go."

Tatiana nodded and began walking beside him.

They headed toward the main building of the Shadow Lounge together.

Up close the place looked even larger than it did in pictures. The structure was built like a luxury hotel with tall glass windows reflecting the night lights outside. The entrance doors were wide and elegant, and a soft golden glow spilled from inside onto the walkway.

People were already arriving for the party.

Some men wore expensive suits while others had the rough confident look of gang members who clearly carried influence in this city. A few women walked in glamorous dresses with heels clicking against the polished ground as they laughed and chatted with their companions.

Tatiana could feel several eyes briefly glance toward the bright orange Lamborghini behind them.

Then those same eyes shifted toward Liam.

But nobody approached.

Nobody stopped them.

They simply watched quietly as the pair walked inside.

The lobby of the building looked exactly like a luxury hotel reception area. Marble floors stretched across the entire hall and modern chandeliers hung from the ceiling casting soft warm light everywhere.

Behind the reception desk stood a well dressed young woman.

The moment Liam approached the counter she straightened her posture slightly.

"Good evening sir."

Her tone was polite and professional.

Liam reached into his pocket and placed the green access card on the counter.

The receptionist picked it up and swiped it through a small device beside the computer.

The machine made a short confirmation beep.

She glanced at the screen quickly.

"Room twenty five is just upstairs," she said with a respectful smile.

Then she stepped slightly forward.

"Do you need me to show you the way, kind sir?"

Tatiana noticed the tone immediately.

The woman was not just being polite.

There was clear respect in her voice.

Liam shook his head calmly.

"No."

His voice remained relaxed.

"I can find it myself."

The receptionist nodded quickly and handed the card back to him.

"Of course sir."

Liam picked up the card and turned away from the desk.

Tatiana followed him silently toward the hallway leading deeper into the building.

But inside her mind the same question kept repeating again and again.

Who exactly is this man?
