‘Not exactly the life of the party, huh.’

I couldn't say for sure, but I felt like the one who wanted to run away the most right now wasn't Second Life Commander Jin or First Life Commander Jin but Kim Hyun-Sung.

He genuinely wanted to dance with me; that was all. I didn’t know why someone who had never really danced before suddenly asked for my permission to dance, but there had to be a reason behind it.

Perhaps the empire’s nobles had been pressuring him behind the scenes. He had always been a passive participant, after all.

If I were to make an educated guess, I'd say he needed a suitable partner to make it seem like he was sincerely engaging in the marriage alliance, and Peneloti, whom he had exchanged letters and talked with, appeared to be a decent choice.

‘Or maybe he genuinely liked her...’

I had no idea what kind of change of heart Kim Hyun-Sung had gone through, but what mattered was that this whole thing took a lot of courage for him.

He was not exactly the most social person out there. Asking someone to dance could seem like nothing to most people, but for Kim Hyun-Sung, it was a historic event.

To make things worse, he didn’t have the communication skills to handle a sudden interruption or a verbal clash with Marquis Jayce. He couldn’t shout that it was rude, couldn’t ask what was going on, and couldn’t even choose the right response.

All he could do was blink blankly and stare at Marquis Jayce.

The three of us stood frozen, saying nothing for what felt like forever.

Perhaps it was only a short moment, but to some people, even a few seconds could feel like a long time. For Kim Hyun-Sung, it had to feel like an eternity. Could someone please just say something? My arm was starting to hurt...

‘What am I supposed to do when both of them are broken?’

At this point, Lady Peneloti had no choice but to step in.

“W-what are you doing, Marquis Jayce?” I asked, freeing Kim Hyun-Sung from his suffering.

Marquis Jayce and Count Kim Hyun-Sung finally came to their senses.
