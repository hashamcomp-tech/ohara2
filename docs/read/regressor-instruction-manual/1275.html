The change was so intense that I almost thought the best option would be to summon Rafael right away and have him beat them all to death on the spot.

I wasn’t sure if they’d like the menu I offered, but it seemed like it really suited their tastes. Of course, I expected some changes, but their transformation was way beyond what I had imagined.

‘This is how things are going to turn out? I didn’t even do anything.’

This was a completely different case from the Templars of Altanus, like Bahamut, Simon, or the one from my memories, Templar Genny.

I knew that Altanus’ blood had powered them up, but it was the result of a long, drawn-out process. They had to have refined and distilled Altanus’ blood before consuming it in the proper, most efficient way through their own rituals.

That was a long time ago when alchemy had yet to develop, so they weren't that advanced at the time. Moreover, they were a clandestine group working in the shadows.

It was common sense that a refined and improved version would be more effective and would have fewer side effects than drinking the raw essence straight up.

Despite the low quantity and low purity, the changes they displayed were so impressive that they left me genuinely bewildered.

‘Damn it, what do I do?’

In fact, a radiant glow was shining behind them.

Watching this scene unfold, I couldn’t help but feel that I was too negligent and careless in my duty as the god overseeing this continent. It looked like the wounds I had suffered after arriving here were now being etched into Jung Jin-Ho’s body.

Jung Jin-Ho gnashed his teeth and clutched his abdomen. It was the exact same spot where Kim Hyun-Sung stabbed me to death.

I was sure Jung Jin-Ho was experiencing the same pain I experienced at the time. He was inheriting not only the memories buried in my blood but the suffering that came with it.

In my eyes, they were distant memories, but the system that governed all dimensions had turned every moment of my past into something like an unskippable story. I thought it would be a simple power-up, but I made a grave miscalculation.
