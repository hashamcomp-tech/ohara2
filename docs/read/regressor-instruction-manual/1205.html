He focused more on Rohen, but what was important to him were the valuable resources in Rohen rather than its people.

Since he would be discarded if he stepped out of line, Woo Hyo-Yeol’s development was important to me as well. Obviously, I had to ensure that he wouldn't become too powerful. If Lee Ki-Young believed that he was a threat, he would be deleted right then and there. He didn’t want Woo Hyo-Yeol to become stronger than Kim Hyun-Sung.

He already knew that was impossible, but I was sure he felt uncomfortable knowing there was someone who could be a threat to his strongest card.

Kim Hyun-Sung had to be the strongest, and his authority had to remain unchallenged.

‘Of course, anything unrelated to combat is excluded.’

Analyzing myself from a third-person perspective was kind of awkward, and at the very least, the version of Lee Ki-Young inside me had those thoughts deep in his heart.

‘Actually, I feel like it’s meaningless to worry about it...'

Woo Hyo-Yeol would never be stronger than Kim Hyun-Sung.

Even if he were to grow just enough to follow Kim Hyun-Sung's shadows, I’d already call that a success.

He had to become powerful enough that he couldn't be discarded, but he couldn't become so powerful that he was a threat.

‘That's perfect.’

Regardless, I got another card I could use.

Of course, I still couldn’t figure out the path I needed to take because his exact goal was unknown to me. Naturally, I felt frustrated because we were making preparations without knowing what kind of test we were going to take.

Despite knowing that it was going to be a treacherous path, we had to take that path, making it an unbearably difficult situation.

The choice available to me was the first one.

‘That’s the reason behind my existence, and he's probably expecting me to do that as well...’

The latter could be easier, but when thinking about the future, it was better to lean toward the former more.

I wouldn’t want to acquire a failing company amidst its fall into the abyss. It was only natural for anyone to take it after things had stabilized. It was highly likely that the fate of Rohen would be determined by how I handled and dealt with it.

The Demon King of Sunset wasn’t the only enemy.

There were two demon lords, the rift, and some GENIUSES.

I wasn’t sure whether Kim Hyun-Sung had intentionally descended here or not, but the Demon King of Sunset’s Castle was kind of a trial. It was to see if the key figures, who would lead Rohen in the future, were worthy of leading it.

It was a trial to see if they could settle down here and carry out their roles properly, even without me.

If they couldn't do that, then Rohen would become a playground for the demons. Lee Ki-Young would probably lead the remaining demon lords on Rohen and tear Rohen apart before swallowing it whole.
