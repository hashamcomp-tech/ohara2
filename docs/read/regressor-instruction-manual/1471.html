‘Okay. This should be enough.’

Of course, it didn’t mean the young ladies could completely capture and take out all the Brigade members. The difference in strength had been considerable from the start, and it was hard to expect the entire tide of battle to turn just because Brush was granted the class called "Devotee of the Black Rose."

Still, for an Epic rank, it was a cost-effective and fairly well-made class, so it was satisfying enough, but the problem was the person using it. Brush was never really skilled in combat to begin with. Even if she were, wouldn’t it still take her quite a bit of time to adapt to a new class?

Of course, it wasn’t just her. It also served as a kind of motivation for the other young ladies.

The best phrase to use to describe the current situation was that things had evened out. Wasn’t it common knowledge that the presence of a priest would make a huge difference in battle?

Unlike the Murder Brigade, who had Sun Hee-Young, the young ladies had limited ways to recover from injuries. They had already reached the limits of what they could restore through cheap potions.

On top of that, Sun Hee-Young was even raising the dead, and for some reason, those corpses seemed capable of using a portion of their original strength.

Fortunately, Lady Brush went through some kind of awakening, so even if she couldn’t carry the fight, she would clearly become a foothold supporting the others. As a bonus, she could handle the undead, too. The young ladies stared at Brush with surprise and nodded once more.

It looked like they thought victory had come to them.

‘My young ladies seem like they're getting pretty carried away.’

To put it bluntly, their faces made it seem like they hadn’t even considered the possibility of defeat. Well, they believed Peneloti was with them, so it was only natural they would look like that, but it still felt like they were placing an almost embarrassing amount of trust in her.

Of course, I wasn’t particularly worried. Normally, excessive confidence tended to have a negative effect in battle, but the young ladies right now weren’t like that.

They were squeezing out every last bit of strength they had and throwing themselves into the fight. It wasn’t arrogance or conceit, but conviction, and that wasn’t the only good sign.

The Brigade members were probably getting headaches by now. There was no real reason for the Brigade to keep this fight going. It was fine as a bit of entertainment at first, but there was no reason to seriously try to kill each other.

Well, perhaps the members wouldn’t mind spending more time here with the young ladies, but at least the ones at the top of the brigade wouldn’t be pleased about how messy things had become.

They'd regret leaving the remaining pieces behind, but there was no reason to keep rolling the dice any further.

When I remembered that Jung Jin-Ho was the founder of the brigade in the first place, the answer became obvious—the only reason he obeyed their instructions was that he personally considered them reasonable.
