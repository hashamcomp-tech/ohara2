Honestly, I felt glad that we brought the seemingly useless peace delegation along with us.

The sight of people singing songs of peace from every direction while the anti-peace treaty protesters responded like they were performing some kind of mass exorcism ritual was truly something to behold.

Even I couldn’t adapt to the atmosphere, so I could only imagine how Kim Hyun-Sung and the others felt at the sight.

‘Well... maybe Hyun-Sung’s the only one looking uncomfortable.’

Jung Ha-Yan clung tightly to my side while we walked, so she didn’t even count. Honestly, she looked completely uninterested in the protesters altogether.

Han Sora, on the other hand, seemed a little nervous because of Jung Ha-Yan, but judging by how much effort she had put into dressing up compared to normal, it looked like she was more worried about the pictures of herself that would end up in the media.

The Blue Guild members were basically celebrities beyond celebrities at this point, so it was completely normal for photos of their daily outfits and fashion choices to circulate all over Benigoa Net. Incidentally, the person constantly showing up in the worst outfits wasn’t Park Deok-Gu but Jo Hye-Jin, who had an obsession with the color blue.

She had ranked in the Top 5 Worst Dressed list in gossip magazines more than once. Han Sora, meanwhile, had previously ranked in the Top 5 Best Dressed alongside Jung Ha-Yan, so it looked like she’d gone all out preparing herself today, too. She kept her posture perfectly straight and maintained a constant smile to avoid getting photographed in an embarrassing way.

Of course, she wasn’t only worrying about herself either. She was obviously checking Jung Ha-Yan repeatedly, too; she was probably afraid that she could accidentally get caught in some humiliating photo as well.

‘Ha-Yan looks cute no matter what angle you photograph her from, so there’s really no need to worry about that.’

Naturally, the supreme being of this district, Kim Hyun-Sung, transcended concerns like embarrassing photos altogether. Honestly, I wasn't really worried about some humiliating pictures or becoming a part of the worst-dressed rankings as long as I was with him. Instead, I was worried about whether some weird rumors would start spreading throughout the media outlets again.

“There are... more people here than I expected,” Kim Hyun-Sung commented.

The way he moved was stiff, making him look like some creaking robot. His expression was distorted, which was even worse than I’d expected. He rarely showed himself in public.

In addition, he was the type to become irrationally anxious whenever he ended up surrounded by too many people, so it made sense that this situation was uncomfortable for him. Spending too long on battlefields probably gifted him with unnecessary anxiety even in situations like this.

“There seems to be... even more people than I thought. P-Perhaps it would be better if we entered the estate by carriage instead...” Kim Hyun-Sung suggested.

“Of course, we can use a carriage if we want to, but this is supposed to be a symbolic moment. Naturally, we need to show our faces to the public. You’re feeling a little nervous, aren’t you?” I asked.

“N-no, it’s not that I’m nervous or anything. I’m just worried that there may be enemies mixed into the crowd,” he answered.

Naturally, quite a few of them started throwing questions directly at Kim Hyun-Sung. Since they were probably convinced from the start that I wouldn’t give the media any useful information, it honestly felt like more reporters were rushing toward him instead.

Originally, I’d planned to walk a little more slowly, make eye contact with the crowd, calm the people down, and then enter the estate, but thanks to this idiot, the whole plan fell apart.

Without giving any kind of performance at all, we ended up standing directly in front of Commander Jin’s estate.

The one saving grace was that the moment I saw Commander Jin’s estate, I instantly forgot the embarrassing disaster Hyun-Sung had just shown to the reporters.

Up until a second ago, I felt like I was stripped naked in public, but the moment I stared at the massive palace in front of me, my mind went blank. To exaggerate a little, it honestly felt like standing before some historical monument.

‘This place is unbelievable...’

“...”

‘The taste really suits that bastard perfectly, too.’

One honestly wouldn’t be blamed for wondering whether this place was in China or the Republic. No, actually, I wasn’t even sure if the word "estate" was the right word to describe that thing before me.

I already knew the guy had a warm heart full of love for his homeland, but what stood before us looked less like a mansion and more like some Chinese emperor’s private palace.

In fact, it was even bigger than the Blue Guild headquarters. Even at a glance, the sheer scale of the property was overwhelming. Even Kim Hyun-Sung, who normally had zero interest in things like house size or luxury, looked genuinely stunned.

‘Does this bastard just have money rotting away somewhere or what? Damn...’

My commoner mindset briefly kicked in. For some reason, just stepping into this place felt intimidating. It felt like finding out that one's neighborhood buddy was secretly the son of some absurdly wealthy family all along. Of course, I had more than enough gold stacked away myself, but people always said that true wealth would reveal itself in the little details and the mindset behind them.

Perhaps for Commander Jin, living in a palace massive enough to make ordinary people uncomfortable was just everyday life. Honestly, I had expected as much...

‘That bastard was definitely filthy rich back on Earth, too.’

Kim Hyun-Sung, with his successful business background, already gave off the atmosphere of a rich heir, but Commander Jin was clearly on an entirely different level of wealth.

Just looking at Jung Ha-Yan nervously shrinking beside me made that obvious enough.

“I-It’s huge, oppa...” Jung Ha-Yan commented.

“I-It really is... Seeing it up close makes it look even bigger... The thing I thought was a park from far away... so it was all Commander Jin’s... house,” I said in agreement.

‘People like us naturally feel intimidated by this kind of thing.’

At least, I was somewhat used to luxury and extravagant parties by now, so it bothered me a little less, but Jung Ha-Yan definitely wasn’t the same as me. She had grown up dirt poor, so honestly, she would probably feel more comfortable at some cheap Big Boy hambier skewer stand than in Commander Jin’s estate.

Even so, I had already taken her to plenty of nice places, and Han Sora also tried exposing her to all sorts of experiences and environments, but the scale of this absurd palace was so overwhelming that all those experiences suddenly felt meaningless.

Even Han Sora herself looked visibly tense. Actually, I felt like Han Sora was more affected than all of us.

She wasn’t like Jung Ha-Yan, who simply walked her own path without caring about others, nor did she have the semi-rich heir aura that Kim Hyun-Sung naturally carried.

Han Sora was just... an ordinary person. It honestly looked like she had no idea how to react based on how she was checking her clothes again and again.

The atmosphere was tense enough that nobody would find it strange if she were to leave right there and then.

Just then, the enormous gates of the estate slowly creaked open. The gates were so ridiculously huge that they looked painfully slow to open, and the one who appeared beyond the fully opened gates was, naturally, Commander Jin. Then...

‘Wh-What the hell... man.’

There were retainers everywhere, and the craziest part was that every single one of them was dressed in clothing straight out of his homeland. For a second, I genuinely thought that I had traveled back in time to some ancient era. Men and women alike were all wearing qipaos.

‘This jerk... Does he have a thing for Chinese outfits?’

Well, a place this massive would truly need an absurd number of retainers to maintain it, but even so, the sheer number of them was ridiculous. Some of them were obviously highly trained fighters, too. Honestly, I barely even noticed Commander Jin.

Behind him was something that looked either like a lake or a pond with a small but absurdly luxurious-looking boat floating on top of it. The landscaping was so beautiful it bordered on insanity. As ridiculous as it sounded, this place honestly looked more impressive than the so-called Three Scenic Wonders we passed by on the way here.

‘Would he let me live here too?’
