Chapter 1452. Continental War (32)

‘Where did Chang-Ryeol go... Why is that guy standing over there like that?’

I was briefly flustered by how intact Ryu Han looked at first glance, but when I stared at him with Mind’s Eye, I saw that he was injured.

I didn’t know whether he recently met a priest for treatment or had healed himself with potions, but I could still see some lingering fatigue and injuries on him. It was also obvious that severed parts of his body and areas that had taken severe damage were forcibly reattached or healed rapidly.

On the surface, he looked completely healed, but damage of that sort could never truly heal, even if it appeared to be healed. Tanks with high Stamina and Endurance recovered relatively quickly, but ordinary frontline fighters needed sufficient rest.

What stood out most of all was the fact that he was currently poisoned. The poison had to have come from a certain someone.

‘He’s the pride of the Blue Guild. It’s the work of Blue Guild’s assassin, Chang-Ryeol.’

It wasn’t poison strong enough to cause death, but he was taking continuous damage from it. It looked like he had tried to detoxify it, but there was no way these primitive first life guys could neutralize Chang-Ryeol’s poison.

Lee Chang-Ryeol himself was well-versed in toxins, and he had probably refined the samples I occasionally handed over to him, so even I didn’t have a complete grasp of what kinds of poison he possessed or how many.

Of course, I knew the one thing all of his poisons had in common—ordinary cure spells or ordinary antidotes would never work on them. If one wanted to detoxify them with a cure spell, one needed Saint-level holy power, and antidotes would always be useless unless they were made by me or by someone highly skilled in alchemy.

Given the situation, even if Lee Chang-Ryeol had used a relatively weak poison on him, there was no way he could detoxify it. At best, all he could do was reduce the pain.

‘Chang-Ryeol... looked like he was really angry.’

“...”

“...”

It seemed like Lee Chang-Ryeol had left him with something quite painful. It looked like a neurotoxin, and the only reason that the killing machine wasn’t crying out in agony was because of his condition.

If it were anyone else, they'd be screaming as if they were being ground to pieces. As expected, losing an arm and being wounded on the side had to have dealt a serious blow to his pride, and the fact that it happened right in front of me probably made him even angrier.

Even if the reason behind his injuries was Alps and Sung Ji-Hoon, Lee Chang-Ryeol’s pride was still injured.

‘Still, he does seem to be alive.’

Ironically, the moment I confirmed Ryu Han’s condition, I felt a bit more certain that Lee Chang-Ryeol was somewhere out there, alive and well.

Of course, Lee Chang-Ryeol was probably not yet in a state where he could decide whether to kill the guy or let him live, but if he truly had no leeway, he wouldn't have left him alive.

If it had been a truly urgent situation, Ryu Han would have long melted away from the poison. Leaving poison like that behind was, for him, a form of petty revenge.

‘Though calling it petty revenge feels like it'll hurt him a lot...’

There had to have been a reason he had to retreat midway. It was probably around the time the Republic forces were pouring into the 4-2 Front. He had to have decided to hide himself for the time being, and while he was at it, he poisoned Ryu Han as well.

As a result, Ryu Han ended up benefiting from the troops entering the 4-2 Front. Of course, it didn’t seem like he was moving together with the troops right now, but unfortunately for us, his target still hadn’t changed, and it even looked as though the other Republic soldiers were finally starting to become aware of us as well.

What we thought were loaches turned out to be massive serpents, and there was no way they would just leave them to run rampant in their own front yard.
