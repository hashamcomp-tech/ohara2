Chapter 1144: A Slightly Early Timing (2)

‘Why are they here?’

A wave of indescribable nervousness struck me out of nowhere.

I was sure most of the people here had no idea who they were or how strong they were, but they probably had an idea.

There wasn’t a single ant in this ruined city, and three angels abruptly descended from the sky.

It was an unusual and incomprehensible sight, but it was easy to assume that they were responsible for turning Harizel into this state. Moreover, they had descended as if they were the mid-boss of a dungeon.

“A-Angels?” Lim Chae-Ryeong mumbled in a daze.

‘They are technically angels.’

Her reaction was only natural, as they actually looked like angels. They had pure white wings, delicate faces, and youthful features that made them look like the very angels humans had imagined. They were also exuding the purity of angels.

‘They’ve grown a lot.’

They looked like elementary school students when I left, but now, they looked like they were in their teens. Children truly grow fast. I saw Thronus expressing hostility with that teenage face of his.

Lim Chae-Ryeong seemed to have finally realized that Woo Hyo-Yeol was attacked just a few moments ago, as she took a step backward and took out her dagger.

Raymond Bolt also got ready to fight.

They labeled them as enemies before questioning where they came from and why they were here. Everyone was fully aware of the fact that they were enemies.

The sweat on Nam Goong-Seon’s face rolled down to her cheeks and dropped to the ground. They were kids, but the party members were aware that they had encountered beings who had transcended human limits.

“W-Why… isn’t… S-Sera here…” Jung Ha-Yan mumbled strange words with her head lowered, while the non-combatant No Dam-Hye just collapsed on the ground.

It was a tense situation where a battle could break out at any moment.

And that was when Cheru muttered, “Father.”

“...”

“Father.”

I pretended that he was a stranger to me. I widened my eyes, and I acted as if I was asking if he was referring to me. I acted kind of scared. I stepped backward and bit my lips, looking wary of them.

Of course, Cheru was disappointed.

Based on the situation, it seemed like he had already heard what happened, but…

‘Is he crying?’

He seemed to have a hard time controlling his emotions, and it wasn't that strange, as he still had the mind of a child. It wasn’t just Cheru. Thro and Domi frowned in surprise as well.

“We already knew this, so don’t be upset, Thro,” Cheru said.

‘Don’t say that when you have tears in your eyes, Cheru.’

“Okay.”

“Auntie is here as well. Is it possible with just the three of us?” Domi asked.

“I’m sure her powers have been sealed, so shouldn’t it be enough? The three of them are weak and the remaining two… don’t look that strong either,” Cheru answered.

“Do you think she lost her memories as well?” Domi asked.

“I don’t know, but she’s likely listening to our father’s words,” Cheru replied.

“What about trying to convince her? If we talk to her—”

“We don’t have time for that. Father is here. You don’t think we have the advantage, do you? Our opponent is our father. Don’t forget that,” Cheru interrupted Domi.

“Father lost his memories. He's our father, but…”

“We still need to stay on our toes. Father is…”

‘What are they whispering about?’

“But… is it really okay to do this? To father…” Thro asked.

“Don’t think like that, Thro! We’re not aiming our swords at him. We’re fighting Rohen, the continent that took Father away from us. Our mothers and Master agreed to this…” Cheru retorted.

“...”

“So keep your guard up,” Cheru added.

‘You don’t have to keep your guard up.’

“What about… the others?” Thro asked.

‘What do you mean? You guys are being nerfed.’

The four Outer Gods were prohibited from killing humans, at least until Ji-Hye noona and I had come to an agreement.

“It’s kind of complicated. Auntie is here… so I can’t use anything powerful,” Domi answered.

“Okay, then don’t overdo it and just support us from the back,” Cheru instructed her.

“Okay.”

‘Shouldn't we come up with a plan like what they're doing?’

There wasn’t a need to do that because our main objective was escaping this place, and the one giving out orders would be. Of course they would overestimate me, but no matter how much I thought about it, we were likely going to lose this fight.

Woo Hyo-Yeol could match their moves somehow, but…

‘The remaining three are the issue.’

If Domi really gave it her all, Jung Ha-Yan wouldn't be able to protect the other three.

Raymond Bolt, the veteran, would turtle up to ensure that the formation wouldn't shatter.

Even if those kids couldn't perform as well as during their peak… it appeared as though they weren't being nerfed. However, I had an answer as to why they could wander outside the Ashen Sunset Region.

‘So you guys got visas as the Outer Gods and snuck in here.’

I wasn’t surprised by that, as they sneaked into our continent as well.

What surprised me was that they were deployed as reconnaissance agents.

It wasn’t a bad choice if one considered it as an effort to expand their influence outside the Ashen Sunset Region. They were still young, but they were capable of making reasonable decisions, except for Sera.

Of course, there was no need for me to mention their strength.

Domi's teleportation, which was her special power, was suitable for this mission.

‘And Ha-Yan can hold off the chase…’

If I were to analyze each and every one of them, I believed everyone could get out of this alive, but I wondered if we really had to do that. After all, Woo Hyo-Yeol’s party needed to experience defeat. My time in Rohen was short, and I didn’t have a lot of time to work with Woo Hyo-Yeol.

Every episode was important, so sudden events like this were important for the story.
