Chapter 1155. Operation: Dawn (4)

— Mr. William.

“Yes?”

— I sent you the coordinates. Focus only on my words from now on. You don’t need to think about anything else.

“Mr. Lee Ki-Young, there's something in front—”

I saw a black energy rushing upward.

‘He’s telling me to ignore that?’

A direct impact could leave the command center, where Mr. Lee Ki-Young was located, partially destroyed. Instructing me to ignore it was easy, but I couldn’t do that, so my hand swung my sword toward the black energy before I could even think of anything else.

It could be said that I ignored his command.

When I came back to my senses and was about to give an excuse, I heard his voice again.

—

Haa...

Logically, the situation was hard to accept.

What exactly did he want me to do?

I was bothered by the unfamiliar way of communication.

Whoo...

why is he doubting so much? He should just do as he’s told. This fool is more disappointing than I thought.

“When you say a test...” I asked.

I looked forward and saw two groups fighting each other. Swords and spears were clashing before me, and I heard the screams of both humans and monsters. I was witnessing a war—a battlefield was right in front of me.

— Can you start heading that way?

“What?”

— Close your eyes and walk.

“...”

— I know my request is kind of strange, but this is important to me. I want to trust you as well, Mr. William. We don’t have time for me to explain every little detail. So... it starts now. Can you do that?

I recalled Mr. Lee Ki-Young’s actions during the mock battle, but this was different. Back then, he was making sure of the familiar terrain, but now, I was standing on a battlefield full of unknown variables.

Despite the hint of uncertainty, I didn’t open my eyes because I trusted him, so I took a deep breath and continued walking.

Boom!

Doubt turned into certainty, and certainty turned into loyalty.

‘Wh-What is this?’

I couldn’t understand it.

The things that were happening were things I just couldn’t understand with common sense. I walked as he instructed, and I felt someone unleashing a massive spell again, but I still walked without stopping.

My body reacted to it at first, but I knew there was no need for me to react.

As expected, the demons' attacks missed me.

The unpleasant clumps of poison that the demons spewed missed me as well.

The enemies' attacks couldn't land on me at all. They were all blocked by debris from a crumbling temple, a magic barrier, and the vanguards.

‘What the hell... is going on? What... kind of magic is this?’

It was to the point that I thought he possessed some kind of strange type of unique ability. Perhaps it was a type of holy power I couldn't see or feel. Perhaps he had some kind of supernatural power. I felt like that was why their attacks couldn't land on me.

Obviously, I knew that wasn’t the case.

However, all I could think about was his voice.

— Do you trust me now?

“No, it’s not that... I doubted you... Mr. Lee Ki-Young...” I answered.

— I’m glad. Then, can we get started now?

The voice in my head confirmed that the path I was taking was the right path.

My feet gradually moved faster, and the scenery around me peeled away.

For some reason, my steps felt light, and it felt like only Lee Ki-Young and I were the only ones who existed on the battlefield. At some point, I stopped hearing the noises around me. Actually, it was more accurate to say that I could no longer hear it.

That was because I couldn’t focus on anything else other than his voice...

I couldn't hear my footsteps, my sword, and the enemies' heads flying into the skies.

I saw the enemy casting spells, but there was no need for me to stop or dodge their attacks.

“...”

[You dare try to challenge Chalkis of Flesh and Blood, you ignorant fool?]

‘It’s possible with Mr. Lee Ki-Young.’

The demon that once looked massive now looked small in my eyes.
