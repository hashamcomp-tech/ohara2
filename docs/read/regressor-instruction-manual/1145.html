Chapter 1145: A Slightly Early Timing (3)

‘W-Who should I sacrifice?’

I had to look around slowly. No Dam-Hye was excluded since she was a promising talent with a bright future, so I could only consider either Lim Chae-Ryeong, Raymond Bolt, or Nam Goong-Seon.

There was no fixed order when it came to death, but Raymond Bolt, the party’s reliable elder, seemed like the right choice. He was someone I had to get rid of someday, and if the team’s oldest member ended up dying, the impact could be quite great.

I obviously considered many possibilities, but despite that, it wasn’t easy to make a decision.

What about Lim Chae-Ryeong, who was starting to build an indescribable relationship with Woo Hyo-Yeol? Sacrificing Nam Goong-Seong to ensure Lim Chae-Ryeong's awakening wouldn't be a bad choice, either.

After much deliberation, I ultimately decided to postpone the decision.

‘It’s better than making a careless decision.’

I was actually thinking that it was still too early. The party needed more time to truly become one, and the party members had yet to build a deep relationship with each other. Sacrificing someone using a poorly constructed story would only create tension between them, which would eventually lead to mistrust.

‘Trust them.’

'Yeah, I’ll trust the party members whom I chose myself. Whether it’s one or two, someone is bound to get hurt, so I’ll just leave it to fate.'

A master playwright wouldn't make characters do things; they would simply guide them to act naturally. There was also the saying that characters tended to move on their own, and there was no guarantee that something like that wouldn't happen in Woo Hyo-Yeol’s party.

“

Kgh!

”

Despite bringing Thronus to a battlefield that was advantageous for the party, the battle was still difficult.

Raymond Bolt blocked another attack and lost his balance again.

Thronus couldn't swing his sword properly, but Raymond still had a hard time blocking it.

Nam Goong-Seon was also getting exhausted mentally and physically because she had to focus her healing on him with each attack.

Jung Ha-Yan was doing the best out of all of them, but fighting back against Dominions’ interference while making sure that Thronus couldn't move freely was tiring her out.

In addition, it seemed like she wasn't really focused, as she was mumbling stuff like, “Why isn’t Sera…”

Hyaaaaaaah!

Just one second—If Grandpa Bolt were one second late in finding his balance, Lim Chae-Ryeong would die at Thronus’ hands.

The training in the dungeon had to have been really effective because the members had gotten pretty skilled. They had to do at least this much with all the enhancement magic and buffs that they were getting, or they would die.

It was undeniable that the party members were putting on an inspiring performance.

“Grandpa, watch your left,” I warned.

“...”

Raymond was running inside the building to try to shake off Thronus’ pursuit, and he held up his shield to his left when I warned him.

Thronus’ sword bounced off Raymond's shield. When it bounced in a different direction, Lim Chae-Ryeong threw her daggers at Thronus.

Considering Thronus' Endurance, it was a ridiculous attack, but Thronus pulled back his sword and slashed out. However, he attacked Jung Ha-Yan’s magic crystals instead of Lim Chae-Ryeong's daggers.

Swooooooosh! Boooooooooom!

The party members ran out of the smoke and into the narrow hallway.

Everyone hurriedly moved to the next location.

“How much longer… pant… pant… do we need to run?” Lim Chae-Ryeong asked.

“...”

“There’s no point in making assumptions. We need to do our best to get out of here,” I told her.

Ugh…

To support Woo Hyo-Yeol, of course.

Ugh

We were at a disadvantage because we were basically cornered.

Unlike Lim Chae-Ryeong, Woo Hyo-Yeol, and Raymond Bolt, who had more stamina than us, Nam Goong-Seon and No Dam-Hye were on the verge of collapse.
