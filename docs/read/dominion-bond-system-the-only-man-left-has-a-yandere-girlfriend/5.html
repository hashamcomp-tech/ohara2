’What the hell is she doing here?’

She gave off a lively, energetic aura.

Compared to her age, her figure was already well-developed. The loose black T-shirt and tight pants she wore gave off a casual, carefree look, though it did little to hide her feminine charm. Her chest pressed lightly against the fabric, giving her an unexpectedly mature presence.

She wore a baseball cap, her dark eyes fixed on the door as she idly swayed the lollipop in her mouth.

Hina sighed inwardly but she was also incredibly happy to see her sister and without noticeming, a small smile played at her lips.

Hina was clearly caught off guard by the sudden hug she received from this clingy little sister of hers, who seemed to have grown a little taller than her.

"Onee-chan!! I missed you." Yuki said still holding Hina tightly afraid she might slip away like always.

Hina could only pat her sister back muttering something to herself.

"Hey, Onee-chan? Why are you still staying in this dummy’s house? Didn’t he die or something?" In her mind it was because of that dummy his sister didn’t have time to spend with her. And she was happy that Reiji had also vanished like the other men, so her sister would have enough time to spend with her.

If only she knew.

Hina expression twisted slightly but she quickly masked it with a soft smile. "Yuki... did you run away from home again?"

"Noo, I ran away from camp and went home. Mom was busy crying her eyes out because dad’s ashes disappeared, poof. So... I used the distraction and tracked you down using her phone." Yuki said causally, before adding in a hurt tone. "Onee-chan why did you block my number?"

Upstairs, Reiji could faintly hear their conversation. At first, he was surprised Yuki had managed to find this place.

But what she said next made his stomach drop. "...Not even ashes?" He muttered to himself. "So when they said ’vanished without a trace’... they really meant it."
