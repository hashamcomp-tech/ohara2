The car roared to a stop in front of my house.

"Thank you for the ride."

’With whose money?’

"Haha, no problem. Drive home safely."

As I waved them off, I turned and opened the door.

*Click*

The door opened with a soft click as I went inside.

I removed my shoes and entered the living room before immediately plopping down on the couch.

"Ahhh..."

I let out a long sigh of relaxation.

Today’s raid was good because of the new member on the team.

The Guild Leader’s daughter.

But she was such a pain in the ass.

"But thanks to her, I got new gear, hehe..."

I said as I looked at my new armor.

I was currently wearing the new standard armor made from tough wyvern scales and light metal, which was not only bulletproof but could also block strong attacks.

It was basically a tank in the form of armor.

Not to mention, the materials were surprisingly light, so they didn’t slow down my movements.

The inside of the suit was lined with something like cushions. I didn’t really know what the material was called, but when I wore it, it felt like I was wearing casual clothes.

It even had a cooling effect that helped keep me cool during intense battles filled with sweat and blood.

I rolled around on the couch, bubbling with excitement.

Though I hadn’t gotten the chance to test the armor in action because the Guild Leader’s daughter insisted on going shopping, tomorrow I was definitely going to try it out.

Thinking about it made me so excited that I rolled too much and fell off the couch.

THUD!

I had just slammed it against the leg of the table.

Today had really been a long day.

But thinking about how I would be sponsoring dinner for the guild team members made another headache rise.

"But with the gear we have now, we could clear gates much faster," I muttered to myself.

"It wouldn’t take long at all. But after spending all the money we’ll get from selling monster cores, I would definitely have nothing left."

A sigh escaped my lips.

"And it might not even take long for her to invite some of our friends to join us, making me go completely broke."

"I really don’t want to go broke..."

Being financially unstable really sucked.

But being completely broke?

Then something crossed my mind.

I suddenly remembered yesterday when the military had come to Reiji’s apartment.

’But really, how was he not detected?’

’Mana scans could pick up even the slightest trace of a living being. Especially if that being possessed mana or had been exposed to someone or something with mana.’

The scene of his back entering the gate replayed in my mind.

I had learned that the awakeners sent to clear the gate never encountered the Boss Monster alive.

’That means he must be at least C-Rank since the gate was at the peak of D-Rank.’

~Ring! ~Ring!

The moment he saw the name, a smile unconsciously appeared on his face.

Hina.

"Hello?"

Silence.

Then a familiar voice spoke softly.

"Rei-kun..."

The smile on his face grew.

"Hina."

It was because she didn’t want anyone overhearing her.
